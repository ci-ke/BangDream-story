event38-06 Chapter 5: Try it Out! Archery

Rinko decides she wants to check out the archery club. She quietly takes aim with her bow, and...

(Character: Sayo Hikawa, Rinko Shirokane)

[Hanasakigawa Girls' Academy - Archery Range]

Sayo: Well then, let's get started.
Rinko: Sorry to ask this of you... I know you said... today was your day off...
Sayo: Don't worry about it. Our advisor was more than happy to let us in once I explained the situation.
Rinko: ... Thank you.
Sayo: I'll start by going over the fundamentals of archery. We can worry about equipment names and such another time.
Rinko: ... O-okay...
Sayo: First, you must understand that archery is not about competing to see who can hit the target. It's a type of martial art where your number one goal is to train your body and mind.
Sayo: The act of drawing and shooting our bow toughens our spirit as well as our muscles. Remembering that is important to archery.
Rinko: I understand... a little... I-I think...
Sayo: I suppose it is a bit of an unfamiliar idea... Let's see... Have you ever heard of the phrase, "Perfect form, perfect shot," Shirokane-san?
Rinko: ... N-no...
Sayo: "Perfect form, perfect shot." An arrow shot correctly will always hit its target.
Sayo: In other words, hitting the target is nothing more than the result of shooting the arrow correctly.
Rinko: So the problem is not... hitting the target... it's learning to how to... shoot the arrow... Right?
Sayo: Exactly. So, would you like to try shooting an arrow today? Firsthand experience really is the best way to learn.
Rinko: Uh... Well... O-okay...
Sayo: Alright. I'll go first, to serve as an example. Keep in mind what we just talked about as you watch me.
Rinko: Alright... I will...
Rinko: (Watching Hikawa-san draw her bow... She looks so... heroic... so cool...)
Sayo: ...
Rinko: ...! You hit the bull's-eye... A-amazing.
Sayo: Thank you. Now it's your turn.
(Black cut)

[Fifteen Minutes Later]

Rinko: Y-you sure need... a lot of strength... to draw the bowstring.
Sayo: That one should offer less resistance... Do you think it'll be too difficult?
Rinko: ... N-no... I can manage... something like this...
Sayo: That's good. Now then, try taking a shot just like I taught you.
Rinko: (First, move my left foot... a half-step... toward the target... Then move my right foot... so that my feet... are an arrow's length apart... Place the arrow... Pull... and release.)
Rinko: (M-my arms... They're shaking...)
Rinko: ...
Sayo: For your first time, that was a beautiful shot... Unfortunately, though, it missed the target.
Sayo: Once more, but this time, try lowering the position of your arrow a bit more before you shoot.
Rinko: ... Lower...? ... Okay.
Rinko: (Same as before... Just position it a little lower...)
Rinko: ...
Sayo: It didn't reach the target. Maybe your grip on the bow is loose...
Sayo: Don't let this discourage you. All beginners have trouble hitting it at first.
Rinko: ... One more...
Sayo: Huh?
Rinko: One more time... Actually... P-please let me... keep trying... until I hit... the target...
Sayo: Shirokane-san.
Sayo: ... Very well. I'll stay with you until you're satisfied.
(Black cut)

[Thirty Minutes Later.]

Rinko: I... I can't... hit it...
Sayo: Next will make your tenth attempt. You're still far off from the target despite having shot so many arrows...
Rinko: ... One more time... Ah!
Sayo: Shirokane-san, are you alright?! ... You cut the top of your thumb...
Sayo: In archery, we rest the arrow on top of our thumbs, kind of like a pillow. It's very common for beginners to get cuts there. I'm sorry... If I had properly explained, this could have been avoided.
Sayo: Let's stop here for today. If we continue, it may negatively impact band activities as well.
Rinko: No... I'm fine...
Sayo: But-...
Rinko: I want to... hit the target. Please... let me do this.

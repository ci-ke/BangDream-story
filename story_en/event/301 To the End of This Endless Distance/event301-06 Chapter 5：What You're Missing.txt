event301-06 Chapter 5: What You're Missing

Sayo opens up to Yukina about her doubts. From Yukina’s eyes, what is it that Sayo still lacks...?

(Character: Yukina Minato, Sayo Hikawa)

Sayo: Sorry to bother you right after practice.
Yukina: I don’t mind. You have something to talk about, don’t you?
Sayo: Yes.
Sayo: Ever since I saw the list of potential venues for our solo live, there’s been something I can’t stop thinking about.
Sayo: What is it that I’m still missing?
Yukina: ...Missing something...
Sayo: Yes. Right now, I should be at the point where I can finally achieve my goal of standing on the same stage as Hina.
Sayo: I’ve become a guitarist capable of playing at venues on the same scale—or even larger—than Pastel＊Palettes’.
Sayo: We’ve even performed at events that only feature bands with major debuts.
Sayo: If you just look at the facts, I could reach that goal anytime... And yet, I still feel like something’s missing.
Yukina: I see...
Yukina: ...That’s unusual. It’s not like you to talk to someone else about something like this.
Sayo: That’s true. Normally, I’d believe that the answer is something I need to find on my own.
Sayo: But this time, the more I think about it, the more it feels like I’m going in circles...
Sayo: That’s why I wanted to hear your objective opinion.
Yukina: ...Alright.
Yukina: Let me ask a few things. Do you feel like you’re lacking in... technique?
Sayo: No... I don’t think there’s any point in comparing my skill to Hina’s to begin with.
Sayo: We’re far too different—both as guitarists and as sisters.
Yukina: Then... is it a vague sense of unease, like you felt when you were struggling with your relationship with Hina before?
Sayo: Not at all. I’ve decided I’ll be Roselia’s guitarist for life.
Yukina: So, you’re not confident in your playing right now?
Sayo: That’s not it either. Ever since I stopped measuring myself against Hina, I’ve been able to focus on my own sound more than ever.
Sayo: ...Although, I might still be hung up on not being satisfied with my performance at All Music Fes.
Sayo: If I’d been happy with how I played at that festival... maybe I wouldn’t be struggling like this.
Yukina: I see...
Sayo: ...I’m sorry. I feel like all I’m doing is telling you “no” to everything.
Yukina: No, I’m just making sure.
Yukina: I didn’t sense any uncertainty or hesitation in your sound, Sayo... none at all.
Yukina: Because of everything you’ve built up until now, your sound isn’t something that can be easily swayed by outside influences anymore.
Yukina: I’m sure you’ve already done everything you possibly could.
Sayo: ...? I’m sorry, but... what do you mean by that...?
Yukina: Sayo... there’s nothing you’re missing in order to reach your goal.
Sayo: ...Eh...
Yukina: I don’t know what you’ll feel when you finally stand on stage with Hina... But I believe you’re ready to step onto that stage.
Yukina: And as for the performance at All Music Fes that came up earlier...
Yukina: Even if you had played perfectly that day, do you really think you’d have said, “Now I’m finally ready to stand on the same stage as Hina”?
Sayo: That’s...
Sayo: (Maybe I’ve just gotten used to taking the long, winding road.)
Sayo: (I’d been gathering clear, undeniable proof—waiting for the day I could finally say, “I’ve done this much, so it’ll be all right.”)
Sayo: (All while not realizing how much closer I’ve already gotten to Hina.)
Sayo: ...Fufufu.
Yukina: ? Did I say something funny?
Sayo: No... I just realized—if you hadn’t said that to me, I probably never would’ve felt like I was ready. Not ever.
Yukina: That might be true. Knowing you, I don’t think you’d ever be fully satisfied.
Sayo: You’re right... I’m really glad I talked to you about this.
Sayo: I used to play the guitar driven by my inferiority complex toward Hina... but by becoming “Roselia’s guitarist,” I was able to come this far—to the point where I can finally stand on the same stage as her...
Sayo: And that’s why I think I wanted to hear those words from you—the one who first started Roselia.
Yukina: ...There were times when I didn’t know who I was anymore, either.
Yukina: But every time, it was you who helped me realize what truly matters.
Yukina: It’s not just because we’ve walked this path together for so long... It’s also the passion I felt from you when we first met—your idealism about music, so much like mine.
Yukina: I’ve believed in that all this time, and I still do. Because they’re your words, I want to believe in them... that’s how I feel.
Sayo: Minato-san...
Yukina: And about making your goal a reality—
Sayo: Yes. There’s something I’d like to discuss regarding that...

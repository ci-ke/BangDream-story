event174-02 Chapter 1: Yoi-no... Miyau-jiyau?

As Hagumi's tutor, Hina must deal with Hagumi's unique perspective...

(Character: Hagumi Kitazawa, Hina Hikawa)

[The Next Day - After School]

[Kitazawa Residence - Hagumi's Room]

Hina: Let's get studying~!
Hagumi: Yeah~!
Hina: Let's start with math since that was your worst subject! I wanna review the problems you got wrong, so can you show me the test you got back yesterday again?
Hagumi: Yeah, sure thing! Here ya go!
Hina: Wow~, I still can't believe how low this score is.
Hagumi: Ehehe~. Yeah, it's kinda embarrassing.
Hina: Well, let's start with this triangle problem. This one's easy. If you use the right triangle similarity theorem, then...
Hagumi: Theo... rem...? What's that mean? Will it change the triangle into a smiley face?
Hina: Ahaha! You're so funny~! That's not it though. Do you have a set square?
Hagumi: Sure do! I've never really used it, though. Here you go!
Hina: Thanks! Let's see, the ratio of the sides of an isosceles right triangle is one to one multiplied by the square root of two...
Hagumi: Oh, no... square roots... I don't like those. Whenever they show up, things always get super hard...
Hina: The square root of a number is the number that, when multiplied by itself, equals the original number. For example, the square root of two is 1.414213562373...
Hina: And the square root of three is 1.73205080756...
Hagumi: Whoa! That was awesome! Did you memorize all that?! How about the square root of four?
Hina: Two.
Hagumi: Huh? ... Just two?
Hina: Hmm. Maybe we should come back to math a bit later. How about we try history next?
Hina: History's not that hard. Just memorize the names and dates of things like shoopidy-doop, and you'll be able to answer questions without even thinking about them!
Hagumi: Really?! Awesome~! All right, I'm ready to study~!
Hina: Here are the questions... Okay, first one! Name the battle where Nobunaga Oda beat Yoshimoto Imagawa when he invaded Owari in 1560...
Hagumi: Ah! I know this one! The kanji for the "o" in Nobunaga's last name, Oda, isn't the kanji for "small." It's the really hard one to write!
Hina: Wow~, you know your stuff. But this question is about the name of the battle...
Hagumi: Why did they fight, anyway? You shouldn't go to war just 'cause you disagree with someone. They should've talked it over and made up!
Hina: It seems like it wasn't something they could just settle with words, and that's why they fought, no?
Hagumi: I don't think that would've happened if Hello, Happy World! had been there! Oda-san and Imagawa-san needed to smile more!
Hina: Ahaha. If Hello, Happy World! had been there, maybe Japanese history would've been different!
Hina: And get this, after this battle, "The Battle of Okehazama," Ieyasu Tokugawa came along and-
Hagumi: Huh? Was he a transfer student?
Hina: ... I guess you could say he was something like that.
Hagumi: Oh! I hope they all became friends!
Hina: ... Hagumi-chan, the way you think is really special. Maybe even more special than I imagined.
Hina: Hmm~. Maybe we should try Japanese Literature next!
Hagumi: Yay~! I'm ready~! Umm... Is this person's name Kusako... Pillow-san?
Hina: Ahaha! It's not a person~. It's the name of a book, "The Pillow Book"!
Hagumi: Ah! I think I've heard of that!
Hina: You probably have. It's like a diary from a person a long time ago! You can read about it in your textbook right there.
Hagumi: Okay! Umm...
Hagumi: The stars are called Subaru, Hikoboshi, Yuuzutsu... Hey, what's this Yuuzutsu?
Hina: There are notes below the text. Take a look.
Hagumi: It says... "yoi-no"... "miyau-jiyau." Is "miyau-jiyau" some kind of... bug?
Hina: Ahaha! I guess it does sound like a bug's name! I like the way you think~.
Hina: It's the Evening Star, and you read it like "yoi-no-myou-jou."
Hagumi: Myou-jou...? But it says "miyau-jiyau" here...
Hina: Apparently, that's how people used to read it back then. Like how the kanji for butterfly, "chou-chou," used to be read as "tefu-tefu," and "kyou," or "today," as "kefu."
Hagumi: "Chou-chou" was read like "tefu-tefu"?! Wow~, I didn't know that!
Hagumi: Now I'm super smart! ... No, super-duper smart!
Hina: Nice~! Now, let's zip through this list of auxiliary verb conjugations and memorize them all~!
Hagumi: Huh~? There's no way I can do that~. If I try to remember them all at once, my brain will melt~.
Hina: Really~? I think it's easy to remember this many.
Hagumi: Hey, why don't we take a break? I'm feeling dizzy. I've been using my brain too much~.
Hina: Hmm. Okay, we could go for a walk!
Hagumi: Yeah, let's go!
Hina: (Well, I guess we should call it day and try to study harder tomorrow~.)

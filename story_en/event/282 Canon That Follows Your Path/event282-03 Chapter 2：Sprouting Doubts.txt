event282-03 Chapter 2: Sprouting Doubts

Mashiro, concerned about Rui, brings it up on their way home—but...

(Character: Mashiro Kurata, Toko Kirigaya, Rui Yashio)

[Walking Home]

Toko: Alright then, I'm heading to the mall after this! See ya!
Mashiro: Y-yeah... see you tomorrow...
Rui: See you tomorrow.
Mashiro: (Tsukushi-chan already left for her part-time job... so it's just me and Rui-san today...)
Rui: ......
Mashiro: (...Th-this is kind of awkward... I need a topic~...)
Mashiro: R-Rui-san, do people ever talk to you about Morfonica at school?
Rui: Sometimes. There are a few who come up to me.
Mashiro: I-I see~...
Rui & Mashiro: ......
Mashiro: (Ugh... I can't think of anything clever to say... At this rate, it'd be better to just go home in silence~...)
Mashiro: (...Even though there's something I should be asking.)
Rui: ...Kurata-san.
Mashiro: Y-yes!
Rui: You don’t need to force conversation. I’m not good at witty responses either.
Mashiro: ...Ah, um, no... that's not it...
Rui: ...?
Mashiro: U-um! I was just wondering... maybe the reason you're having trouble composing is... because of my lyrics...?
Rui: ...Why would you think that?
Mashiro: Well... this time, I kind of just wrote exactly how I felt...
Mashiro: About the Tsukinomori Music Festival... and the Flower Bloom Rock Fest before that...
Mashiro: Once I started writing everything I felt and saw during those moments, there was just so much I wanted to say...
Mashiro: And I thought maybe that’s made it harder for you to turn it into a song...
Rui: ...It’s not because of your lyrics.
Mashiro: R-really...? Then why...?
Rui: ......
Mashiro: Y-you’re not just saying that to spare my feelings...?
Rui: That’s not it.
Rui: ...This is my own issue.
Mashiro: Your own...?
Rui: Yes. ...I don’t know what the right choice is.
Mashiro: Huh...?
Rui: I’ve had trouble composing before, but I always knew the direction or goal.
Rui: Because I had a sound in mind. A sound I could chase after by being in Morfonica.
Rui: ...But during the Tsukinomori Music Festival, I felt it. That I’d finally found the sound I was searching for.
Mashiro: I-Isn’t that a good thing...?
Rui: Yes. In one sense, it’s a very good thing.
Rui: But it’s because I found it... that I’m no longer sure what to aim for next—or where my next destination lies.
Mashiro: ......
Rui: ...Now you understand, don’t you?
Mashiro: Huh?
Rui: That your lyrics weren’t the problem. I told you this because I thought otherwise you’d keep blaming yourself.
Mashiro: ......
Mashiro: I-Isn’t there anything we can do to help?
Rui: ...No. I don’t think there is.
Rui: This is something I need to work through on my own. I don’t intend to tell anyone else about it, so I’d like you to keep that in mind.
Mashiro: O-okay...
Rui: You’re going that way, right? Well then, goodbye.
Mashiro: G-goodbye...

event275-01 Opening: Let’s Go! Backroom Tour!

Kanon visits the aquarium alone for the first time in a while. An unexpected stroke of luck awaits her.

(Character: Kokoro Tsurumaki, Kaoru Seta, Hagumi Kitazawa, Kanon Matsubara, Misaki Okusawa)

[Aquarium - Entrance]

Kanon: (When was the last time I came to the aquarium...? Even on a weekday, there are more people than I expected... Is there some event going on?)
Kanon: (But today’s lecture was canceled, so I’ve got plenty of time to take it slow.)
Kanon: Fufu. I’m really looking forward to seeing Pen-chan again...
Kanon: Kya!?
Aquarium Staff: Congratulations! You are the 10 millionth visitor to our aquarium!
Kanon: Eh...?
Visitors: Wow! Congratulations!
Kanon: ...F-fueeeee~~~!!??
(Black cut)
(Background change)

[Aquarium - Backroom]

Hagumi: Wow! So this is what the aquarium looks like behind the scenes~!
Kaoru: Pipes running everywhere, giant machines... how fleeting it all is...
Aquarium Staff: On this backstage tour, we’ll explain how the aquarium works and what we staff do.
Aquarium Staff: You'll also get to experience some of our jobs, so please watch closely.
All: Okay!
Misaki: It’s pretty amazing. We got invited to the aquarium, and on top of the backstage tour, we even get a job experience.
Kokoro: It’s all thanks to Kanon for being the 10 millionth visitor!
Kanon: Ehehe... I was so surprised, but I’m happy to be here with everyone.
Kaoru: If I recall, the jobs we’ll try are tank cleaning, feeding, and dolphin show staff, right?
Hagumi: Kano-chan-senpai, do you know what you want to do?
Kanon: Hmm... The dolphin show sounds fun, but being in the pool is a bit scary... maybe cleaning the tanks...?
Misaki: Well, we’re just getting started on the tour, so you can decide as you see more.
Kanon: Fufu, you’re right.
Aquarium Staff: Now then, does anyone know what these many pipes are used for?
Misaki: Well, in an aquarium, pipes usually mean...
Hagumi: Yes! I think it’s water!
Aquarium Staff: Correct! If you turn the valve on this pipe, the tank will completely drain, so please be careful.
Hagumi: The tanks would be empty!? The fish wouldn’t be able to swim!
Kaoru: That would be terrible...! I shall engrave it in my heart to protect these fleeting lives.
Kokoro: Yes, let’s make sure not to touch the valves!
Aquarium Staff: Fufu, thank you. Now for another quiz. What do you need to keep fish at home?
Kanon: For keeping fish... first, a tank.
Kokoro: Yeah! And water for the tank too, right?
Misaki: I guess you’d also need an air pump.
Hagumi: Yes! And food, of course!
Aquarium Staff: Great! Anything else?
Kaoru: Hmm... I’d say... love for the fish.
Kokoro: That’s it!
Hagumi: That’s amazing, Kaoru-kun!
Aquarium Staff: Y-Yes, that’s important too...!
Misaki: Come on, you’re confusing the staff. That’s not what they were asking.
Kanon: Hmm... maybe machines to keep the water clean and at the right temperature?
Aquarium Staff: Exactly! Keeping the water clean and the temperature stable is very important for the fish.
Kokoro: I see! That’s just like you, Kanon!
Kanon: Ehehe...
Aquarium Staff: Here, we pump seawater from the nearby ocean, filter it with these machines, and send it to the tanks through the pipes.
Hagumi: Wow! They use real seawater!
Aquarium Staff: Yes. Other aquariums sometimes use artificial seawater made from salts and minerals, or transport seawater by truck.
Misaki: Wow~, I didn’t know that. I never imagined trucks driving around carrying seawater.
Kaoru: I see... even the tank water has its own story depending on the aquarium.
Kokoro: If you can bring seawater by truck, does that mean you could build your own aquarium at home!?
Aquarium Staff: That’s true. In that case, you’d need to set up the same kind of equipment we have here. For example—
Misaki: U-uh, maybe let’s stop there...! If you say any more, she might actually build one.
Aquarium Staff: ?
Kaoru: But dreaming up your ideal aquarium sounds like fun too.
Kanon: Yeah. I think I’d definitely want a jellyfish section.
Hagumi: Kano-chan-senpai, you really love jellyfish!
Aquarium Staff: I see. Then I think you’re going to enjoy the next place I’ll be showing you.

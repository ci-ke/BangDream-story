event296-03 Chapter 2: Custom Dress Challenge!

The group visits Toko’s house to begin designing the dress. As Himari looks through the many designs, she begins to...

(Character: Saya Yamabuki, Himari Uehara, Aya Maruyama, Toko Kirigaya, Rokka Asahi)

[Kirigaya Residence - Kimono shop]

Toko: Come on in, make yourselves at home!
Himari, LOCK, Saya & Aya: Thanks for having us...!
Himari: You said it was a kimono shop, but... wow, it’s even more amazing than I imagined...! You can really feel the weight of tradition here...!
Aya: Yeah, I’ve only seen places like this in TV dramas...!
Toko: It’s nothing special—pretty plain, right? Anyway, today it’s not about my house—it’s all about the wedding dress!
Saya: Right. There’s only two weeks left, do you think we’ll make it in time...?
Toko: Well, normally a custom dress takes two months—sometimes up to six.
Aya: What!? Then there’s no way we can make it...!
Toko: No no, I mean if we did it the normal way!
Toko: I actually just asked someone I know who’s super good at making dresses, and they said they could totally do it in a week!
Toko: So, as long as I crank out the design real quick, it’s not “only” two weeks—it’s a whole “two weeks to work with”!
Himari: Th-That’s amazing...! Wait, is that even possible!? It’s like magic!
Toko: Heh, well, when you’re at my level, this is nothing. Now then, Himari-san, time to take your measurements!
Himari: Huh!? Already!? Can’t we wait until I'm not full—no, don’t pull me~~~!
(Black cut)
Himari: Ugh... I should’ve gone on a diet first...
Toko: Eh~, I don’t think you have anything to worry about!
Aya: Ah! Great job today, Himari-chan, Toko-chan! Hey hey, look at this~!
Himari: Is this... a bridal magazine?
Saya: While you were getting measured, we thought we’d look up some wedding dress designs, so we went out and bought this!
LOCK (Rokka): There are so many more dress styles than I imagined!
Aya: This one’s really common, right? It’s called an A-line, isn’t it?
Toko: Exactly! The skirt flares out like the letter “A,” that’s where the name comes from.
Toko: This one’s super classic, but personally, I think the mermaid silhouette would suit Himari-san more~.
Himari: The mermaid one! That’s the one that’s fitted around the knees and then flares out at the bottom—like a dress that makes you look like a mermaid!
Toko: Exactly! It’d look amazing on you—your figure is perfect for it!
Himari: Eh~, really~? Then maybe I should slim my waist down a bit more first...!
LOCK (Rokka): This dress looks a bit different from the A-line one. It’s more poofy all over...
Himari: That’s called a princess line!
Himari: The skirt flares out gently from the waist—like a fairytale princess, right?
Aya: So cute~. It really catches the eye with all that volume!
Saya: So these are the three main styles, huh... What’s your dream dress, Himari?
Himari: Eh~, they’re all so pretty, I can’t decide.
Toko: Then maybe start by focusing on the features you like the best?
Toko: An A-line dress can make your legs look longer, and a mermaid silhouette gives off a more mature vibe.
Toko: The princess line is super elegant, but it might give off a slightly more classic feel!
Himari: Hmm... I want to look taller, but I also want that elegance~... but I do like the grown-up style too...
Toko: Honestly, any of them would be fine by me! I’m confident I can make the perfect dress out of any of them!
Himari: Woah~, now I’m even more torn~!
LOCK (Rokka): Then~... how about going with the one that made your heart flutter first?
Himari: The one that made my heart flutter... first?
LOCK (Rokka): Yes! When I was picking out my guitar, I thought they all looked great, but the one that made my heart race at first glance sparkled the most to me...
LOCK (Rokka): The store clerk even suggested I go with something more beginner-friendly, but I just couldn’t let go of that one guitar.
LOCK (Rokka): It took a lot of effort and time, but I’m really glad I chose the one that made my heart race! That’s why I think—it might be the same for your dress, Himari-senpai.
Himari: I see... You’re right...!
Himari: Then... I want a princess-line dress...
Himari: It might sound a little childish, but my very first dream was to be a fairytale princess.
LOCK (Rokka): I think that’s a beautiful reason. I’m sure it’ll suit you perfectly.
Aya: Yeah, yeah. I just know you’ll look super cute and beautiful in a princess-style dress, Himari-chan.
Himari: Rokka, Aya-san~...! That gave me so much courage...!
Himari: Toko-chan, let’s go with a princess-line dress!
Toko: You got it! Now, let’s keep the momentum going and sort out the details.
Himari: So, uh... what do we decide on next?
Toko: Like the shape of the sleeves, the back design, the lace pattern... all kinds of things! Only if you have preferences, of course!
Saya: That’s true—even with princess lines, there are so many variations.
Aya: Himari-chan, the magazine has tons of princess-line dresses in it too! Let’s see if we can find one that matches your ideal.
Himari: Right! ...Oh! This one’s adorable~!
Aya: I know, right? I love the shape of the frills! This one has a ribbon, too—but it’s not too sweet, kind of a classy princess vibe~.
Himari: Wow~, you’re right! There are so many choices, I might get stuck deciding... But I have to decide soon...!
LOCK (Rokka): But you’ve got such a big smile, Himari-senpai!
Himari: Fufu, maybe it’s just ‘cause I’m having so much fun! I guess this is what they call a happy problem~.
Saya: Fufu, sounds like it. You might also want to start thinking about accessories to go with the dress.
Toko: Oh! Himari-san, I bet you’d love this kind of hair accessory! It’s delicate but super glamorous—totally princess vibes, right~?
Himari: Whoa, it’s so cute! How did you know exactly what I like!?
Toko: A genius designer can read hearts, you know~ With this, the dress would look great sleeveless—or even with sleeves like this...
Himari: Ugh~~~, everything’s too pretty, I can’t decide~!
Saya, Aya, Toko & LOCK: Ahahaha.

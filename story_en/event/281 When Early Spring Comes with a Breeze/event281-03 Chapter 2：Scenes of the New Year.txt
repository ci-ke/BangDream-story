event281-03 Chapter 2: Scenes of the New Year

After getting advice from Kasumi, the two set off to explore the town in search of something New Year’s-themed.

(Character: Saya Yamabuki, Rinko Shirokane)

[Residential Area]

Saya: There are a lot of houses with kadomatsu and shimekazari out front.
Rinko: Ah... look over there. That house has zodiac figurines on display...
Saya: You're right, they're so cute~. But still, we probably shouldn't take pictures in front of someone else's house...
Rinko: Right...
Saya: Does your family put up any New Year decorations, Rinko-senpai?
Rinko: Let’s see... We usually put up a hamaya inside the house, but that’s about it... How about your family, Yamabuki-san...?
Saya: We go all out. It's important for good luck too.
Rinko: Ah... that makes sense. Since your family runs a business...
Saya: Yeah. Once December hits, we all go to the shrine or downtown markets to get New Year’s decorations together. Though we just buy the kagami mochi from the supermarket.
Saya: One time I mentioned to a guy downtown that I wanted to try making mochi at least once, and before I knew it, they were planning a mochi-pounding event...
Rinko: Fufu... You’re really close with everyone down there, huh...
Saya: Yeah, they’ve looked after me since I was little. They’re basically like extended family to me.
Saya: And since they’ve been so kind to me, I hope I can repay them somehow someday.
Rinko: Ah...
Saya: Rinko-senpai, something wrong?
Rinko: There’s a kite... I think someone must be flying it by the riverbank.
Saya: Oh, you’re right. It really gives off that New Year vibe. It stands out nicely against the blue sky—would make a great shot. It’s a bit far, but if I do a bust-up shot, I think I can fit it in the frame.
Saya: Rinko-senpai, want to give it a try? Could you stand over there and look this way...?
Rinko: O-Okay...
Saya: (Her expression’s still a little stiff... But if I point it out, she might get even more self-conscious...)
Rinko: How is it...?
Saya: Hmm, the kite was a nice touch, but it was still a bit too far.
Saya: Let’s head back downtown and look around for some nice displays we can use for a few more shots.
Rinko: O-Okay...
Saya: Rinko-senpai, that step’s a little uneven—be careful.
Rinko: Ah... thank you... I didn’t notice it...
Saya: Furisode can get pretty heavy, huh? It’s easy to zone out when you’re tired. Let’s take a break soon.
Rinko: .........
Saya: Something on your mind?
Rinko: Fufu... no, it’s nothing...
Saya: ?
(Black cut)
(Background change)

[Park]

Saya: It’s actually kinda hard to find things that really feel like New Year’s and are photogenic.
Rinko: Yeah... we've taken a lot of pictures, but...
Saya: They work fine for our own memories—like a little New Year snapshot collection.
Saya: Look at this one. A shimekazari on a doghouse. And this one...
Rinko: Ah... this one's cute too... That doll at the cake shop dressed in a kimono...
Saya: This one's cute too. Kids playing with spinning tops and hanetsuki paddles.
Rinko: They were all such sweet kids...
Rinko: Hmm... What else feels like New Year...
Saya: ...Hm? Do you smell that?
Rinko: Oh, you’re right... it smells toasty and delicious...
Saya: Ah, it’s that house across from the park. Looks like they’re grilling mochi on a shichirin.
Rinko: That has such a nice vibe...
Saya: Mochi is definitely a New Year staple.
Rinko: It is...
Saya: But as a contest motif, it might lack a bit of impact...
Saya: Sorry, I just can’t seem to decide. I’m probably being too particular about it.
Rinko: Not at all. Having a strong vision is important...
Rinko: Let’s keep looking until you’re satisfied... I’ll do my best too, so we can get a great photo...
Saya: Thank you, Rinko-senpai!
Saya: But I feel like we’ve already combed through most of the New Year’s sights in town...
Saya: ...Ah. What about Michelle?
Rinko: Michelle-san...?
Saya: Yeah, there’s an event at the shopping street during New Year’s, and Michelle was helping out with the raffle.
Rinko: Now that you mention it... I think I heard something like that when Kitazawa-san gave me a charm...
Saya: I don’t know if Michelle counts as “New Year-ish,” but she might be helping out again this year.
Rinko: I see... and we don’t really have other leads right now, so... Shall we head back to the shopping street for now?
Saya: Yes!
(Black cut)

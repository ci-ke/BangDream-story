event281-04 Chapter 3: Everyone’s Own New Year

They returned to downtown to look for Michelle—but the one they met there was...

(Character: Saya Yamabuki, Tomoe Udagawa, Rinko Shirokane)

[Downtown]

Saya: Mm, no sign of Michelle~.
Rinko: The shopping street is still... really bustling.
Saya: Yeah.
Rinko: So, what kind of things does the New Year event here usually have...?
Saya: Lots of stuff, actually. Our bakery is planning to sell bread at the storefront starting tomorrow.
Saya: There’s also things like singing contests and lion dances. Events like those run through the first three days of the year.
Rinko: I heard about the lion dance... from Ako-chan and Asahi-san... They gave me some of your bakery’s bread along with a charm...
Rinko: That chocolate coronet I ate during my exam studies... It was such a comforting taste...
Saya: You even sent me a thank-you message about it. That made me really happy!
Tomoe: Huh? Saya... and Rinko-san!
Saya: Tomoe. Happy New Year.
Rinko: Happy... New Year...
Tomoe: Happy New Year! You two are kind of an unexpected pair.
Saya: Yeah, kind of. Are you headed somewhere, Tomoe?
Tomoe: Just dropping something off at Hazawa Coffee. Then I’m heading to Hagumi’s place to help with the event.
Saya: Sounds like a busy New Year for you.
Tomoe: Well, yeah. But the folks around here have always looked out for us, so I want to return the favor while I can.
Saya: I bet everyone appreciates that. Oh, right—do you know if Michelle came to the shopping street today?
Tomoe: Doesn’t seem like it. It’s New Year’s, so she’s probably got her hands full too.
Saya: I see... Hmm...
Saya: Actually, Rinko-senpai and I are taking photos for the contest right now—
(Black cut)
Tomoe: The downtown photo contest, huh? I bet you’ll do great, Saya. Good luck!
Saya: Fufu, thanks.
Saya: Oh, by the way, Tomoe—when you think of something New Year’s-y, what comes to mind?
Tomoe: Me? Hmm...
Tomoe: Mandarins, maybe...?
Rinko: Mandarins...?
Saya: Feels more winter-themed than specifically New Year’s...
Tomoe: Haha, yeah, fair enough.
Tomoe: But at our place, we usually spend New Year’s just hanging out in the living room, snacking on mandarins. So to me, mandarins go right alongside mochi and osechi as part of the whole vibe.
Saya: Yeah, I can see a lot of people spending New Year’s that way.
Rinko: If that’s how it is... then maybe mandarins are New Year’s-y after all...
Saya: Well, since Michelle’s not here, should we look for some mandarins instead?
Rinko: Let’s do that...
Saya: Thanks, Tomoe. We’ll try the greengrocer.
Tomoe: Got it.
Tomoe: Oh, do you need help at Yamabuki Bakery tomorrow?
Saya: Thanks! But we’re good for now.
Tomoe: Okay. See you around!
Saya: See you.
Tomoe: ...So they’re out hunting for something New Year’s-themed for the photo contest, huh?
Tomoe: Is she seriously planning to enter a photo of Rinko-san with a mandarin...?
(Black cut)
The greengrocer: One mandarin, coming right up. Thanks for your purchase!
Saya: ...
Saya: Sorry to keep you waiting, Rinko-senpai. I got the mandarin, but...
Saya: Um... here it is. The mandarin.
Rinko: Yes... the mandarin...
Saya & Rinko: ...
Saya & Rinko: Ahaha. Fufu.
Saya: Phew, that was funny... even a photo of you in a kimono with a mandarin doesn’t exactly scream “New Year’s,” huh?"
Rinko: We kind of lost the plot somewhere, didn’t we... fufufu.
Saya: Oh, hey—since we’re back near downtown anyway, why don’t we take a break at my place? All that walking in zōri must’ve worn you out.
Rinko: Your house...? Are you sure it’s not a bother...?
Saya: Not at all, I’d love to have you!
Rinko: Thank you very much... Then... I’ll take you up on your offer...
(Black cut)
(Background change)

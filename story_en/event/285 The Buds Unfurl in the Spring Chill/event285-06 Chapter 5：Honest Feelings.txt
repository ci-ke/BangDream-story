event285-06 Chapter 5: Honest Feelings

After resolving to face her feelings and express them properly, Tsugumi calls Ran out to the courtyard.

[Haneoka Girls' Academy - Courtyard]

Tsugumi: Sorry, I know you're busy with your exam coming up.
Ran: It’s fine. You wanted to talk, right?
Tsugumi: Yeah... um, so...
Tsugumi: ......
Tsugumi: I-I'm sorry. Suddenly, I just couldn't find the words.
Ran: ...If you can’t organize it, then just say it as it is.
Tsugumi: Eh, but...
Ran: It doesn’t have to be perfectly worded. I want to hear it in your own words.
Tsugumi: In my own words...
Ran: Ah... I’m not trying to pressure you. Just don’t worry about me so much.
Ran: You always think from the other person’s perspective. When my lyrics weren’t landing, during the exhibit too—you always tried to understand me through flowers.
Ran: You actually touched the flowers, looked up their meanings... and imagined how I felt. You really reached out to me.
Ran: That’s something I really admire about you. It meant a lot. But I think that same kindness might be why it’s hard to talk now.
Tsugumi: Ran-chan...
Ran: So don’t overthink it—just talk. I’m here to listen.
Tsugumi: Okay... Thank you.
Tsugumi: ...Lately, when you talk about flower arrangement, I start feeling... weird inside...
Tsugumi: Like suddenly I can’t talk to you the way I normally would. I didn’t understand why, so I’ve been thinking a lot...
Ran: ......
Tsugumi: I think... I was just feeling lonely.
Tsugumi: Ah, not just lonely... Seeing you work so hard on flower arrangement made it feel like you were going somewhere far away...
Ran: Huh...?
Tsugumi: Ah... I knew it. This kind of thing must be hard to respond to...!
Ran: No, not at all. I just wondered—what made you feel lonely?
Tsugumi: Well... it’s like... it felt like you were leaving us behind somehow.
Tsugumi: We’ve always been together, and I know we’ll stay that way, but... it won’t be quite the same anymore.
Tsugumi: And of course, that’s how things go. You and I both change in order to stay true to ourselves.
Tsugumi: But... the “now” we’ve built together is so precious to me, I found myself wishing it wouldn’t end. And that made my chest ache with this quiet sadness.
Ran: Tsugumi...
Tsugumi: I guess I didn’t want the band to change... or our “same as always” to shift. It felt like saying goodbye to the version of you—and me—that exists right now...
Tsugumi: But at the same time, I really wanted to tell you, “Good luck on your exam,” and mean it from the heart. But those other feelings just kept getting in the way... and I couldn’t say it properly...
Ran: I see...
Ran: ...I thought you were mad at me. After all, my flower arrangement stuff caused a lot of trouble for the band...
Ran: We’ve managed to keep the band going, but that doesn’t mean your feelings just magically got fixed. So I decided—I’d face those feelings of yours properly.
Tsugumi: I-I wasn’t mad!
Ran: But didn’t you feel like that at least once?
Tsugumi: ...Maybe a little.
Ran & Tsugumi: ......
Ran & Tsugumi: Pfft... Fufufu...!
Tsugumi: Kind of late to say all this, huh?
Ran: I could say the same. ...But I’m really glad we talked.
Ran: You don’t usually talk about feeling lonely, or a little mad... Stuff like that.
Tsugumi: I always think it’s too small to bring up, or not worth saying.
Ran: Same here. Actually, maybe I don’t say nearly enough...
Tsugumi: ...You might be right.
Ran: ......Sorry.
Tsugumi: Fufu, but that’s part of what makes you so you.
Tsugumi: I’m sorry too. If we’d talked like this from the start, maybe things would’ve been different.
Ran: Still... I’m glad we’re having this talk now.
Tsugumi: Yeah... You’re right.
Tsugumi: ...Ran-chan. I still really admire you.
Tsugumi: Seeing you keep your eyes forward and run ahead is so cool. But if I keep staring at your back, I’ll feel lonely again, so...
Tsugumi: So this time, I’m going to run full speed down my own path.
Ran: Tsugumi...
Tsugumi: So fast that someday I’ll pass you. So fast that I’ll be the one showing you my back.
Tsugumi: So keep running, Ran-chan. No holding back. Let’s keep being the kind of friends who can say, “Not bad at all.”
Ran: ...Got it.
Ran: I’ll be running full speed too.
Tsugumi: ...Yeah!
Tsugumi: (I'm sure this loneliness won’t go away for a while.)
Tsugumi: (But this feeling is still an important part of my true self. Even so... I’m okay now. Because I know there’s someone who’ll truly listen.)
Tsugumi: Ran-chan, I’m still debating what flowers to put on the counter at the café. Do you have any suggestions?
Ran: On the counter? Like in that driftwood piece?
Tsugumi: Yeah! I want something that’s perfect for this time of year.
Ran: Hmm... Oh, in that case—

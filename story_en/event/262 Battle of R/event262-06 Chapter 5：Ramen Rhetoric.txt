event262-06 Chapter 5: Ramen Rhetoric

Pouring all their feelings into their drumming, Tomoe and MASKING face each other head-on.

(Character: Arisa Ichigaya, Tomoe Udagawa, Hagumi Kitazawa, Yukina Minato, Masuki Sato)

Tomoe: The most important part of ramen is the soup that fills the bowl.
Tomoe: Why do we have tonkotsu, shoyu, shio, and miso flavors?
Tomoe: Because soup varieties are like musical genres. Each has its own tradition, history, and the feelings of the people behind it.
MASKING (Masuki): Sure, soup is important for ramen. I’ll give you that. But still!
MASKING (Masuki): Ramen in kanji means “pulled noodles.” It literally refers to the noodles themselves.
MASKING (Masuki): Ramen, at its core, is the noodle! The noodle is the heart of ramen.
Tomoe: Soup accepts and embraces any topping. It brings everything together—like the drums in a band.
Tomoe: I want to be the kind of drummer who wraps everyone up, like a warm, comforting soup.
MASKING (Masuki): The soup, the toppings—all kinds of flavors come together, and yet it's the noodle that stays true to itself.
MASKING (Masuki): That’s the kind of drummer I want to be—like a noodle with unwavering strength.
Tomoe & MASKING: —That’s why.
Tomoe & MASKING: I... I...
Tomoe & MASKING: Most important is—!
(White cut)
Tomoe: Haah, haah... Yelling while drumming is exhausting...
MASKING (Masuki): Yeah... But I feel a lot better now.
Tomoe: ...Yeah, me too.
Tomoe: Masuki.
MASKING (Masuki): Yeah...?
Tomoe: I get now how much the noodles mean to you. But I can’t back down on how much I care about the soup either.
MASKING (Masuki): I know. Same here.
MASKING (Masuki): ...Then maybe we’re thinking the same thing now.
MASKING (Masuki): It’s not about which is more important. We’ll take my noodles and your soup and make the strongest, ultimate ramen.
Tomoe: Exactly. Let’s make it—the strongest, ultimate ramen!

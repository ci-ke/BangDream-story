[A Few Days Earlier]

[Hazawa Coffee]

Tsugumi: Welcome to Hazawa Coffee. Ah, Sayo-san!
Sayo: Hello. It will just be me, but will that be acceptable?
Tsugumi: Of course! We don't have any other customers right now, so you're welcome to take a seat anywhere you'd like.
Sayo: Thank you very much.
Tsugumi: What would you like to order?
Sayo: Let me think. I would like my usual tea and... Oh, interesting...
Tsugumi: Yes, it's almost Valentine's Day, so we've added some chocolate items to the menu!
Sayo: I see... It is already that time of the year...
Sayo: Time seems to have gone by very quickly somehow.
Tsugumi: Well, there has been a lot going on until recently. You signed up with an agency, and I've been going on band trips...
Tsugumi: By the way, have you decided yet what to do for this year's Valentine's Day chocolate?
Sayo: I intend to prepare something homemade. What about you?
Tsugumi: Me too. I want to make something using chocolate for my band, but...
Tsugumi: I made chocolate tarts last time, and chocolate fondants the time before that... It's just so hard to find something new to give when you've been doing it every year.
Sayo: I would imagine...
Tsugumi: I've been going through cooking websites and checking out the recipes that catch my eye, but nothing's really called out to me.
Sayo: ... This reminds me. There was a chocolate roll cake in a novel I read the other day. It seemed quite delicious.
Tsugumi: Ah, you're talking about "A Dream Gentler Than White Flowers," aren't you?
Tsugumi: There's just something about the cheerful and energetic high school girl main character that makes me want to root for her.
Sayo: Yes, I also feel the same way about her somewhat shy friend who worries about her studies and future... She struck me as a student you could meet in real life.
Tsugumi: And the dessert they would have at the café they always stopped by after school...
Sayo: The hopeful spring chocolate roll cake.
Tsugumi: Yes! That's the one.
Sayo: The walnuts inside are described as having a unique texture that makes them irresistible.
Tsugumi: And ganache that just melts on the tongue the moment you take a bite, spreading pure bliss everywhere... It does seem pretty delicious, doesn't it...?
Tsugumi: Did you know the one in the story is based on a real chocolate roll cake?
Sayo: Is that so?
Tsugumi: There's a café that serves it in the town where the main character lives! The town itself isn't that far from here by train either.
Sayo: You certainly seem to be quite knowledgeable.
Tsugumi: When I was looking for reviews online, I happened to come across a website with this kind of information...
Tsugumi: It sure would be nice to try out the chocolate roll cake though. I might even be able to get some ideas for what I'm making for Valentine's Day.
Sayo: ...
Sayo: ... Would you perhaps like to go together then?
Tsugumi: What?! Ah, sorry, no, I mean, I wasn't suggesting that...!
Sayo: You are not the only one curious about the roll cake, and since I also intend to prepare something, I am hoping there are things for me to learn.
Sayo: What do you think?
Tsugumi: ... Then, yes! I'd love to go! Let's go together!
Tsugumi: Ah, instead of just going there to eat, why don't we also explore the town?
Tsugumi: The café isn't the only place that actually exists. There's their school route, the park, and more... That website had a ton of pictures!
Sayo: ... I am quite intruiged now.
Sayo: How does this weekend sound then?
Tsugumi: Perfect! Fufu, I can't wait.

event293-03 Chapter 2: A Grown-Up Lunchtime?

After finishing their scouting, the two stop by a café they find along the way.

(Character: Nanami Hiromachi, Tsukushi Futaba)

[Live House - Reception]

Tsukushi: Thank you so much for talking with us!
Tsukushi: ...Alright! I think we’ve checked out all the places we wanted to!
Nanami: Wow~, good work~. This one looked pretty nice too, huh?
Tsukushi: Yeah! Thanks for all your help, Nanami-chan. There were things I might’ve forgotten to ask if I were alone, so you really helped me out.
Nanami: No, no~. You already had all the important stuff covered, I just asked about a few details that caught my eye~.
Tsukushi: Fufu, I just hope we can find a place Mashiro-chan will feel excited to perform at.
Nanami: Yeah, yeah. Still, we finished way faster than I expected.
Tsukushi: You’re right. We could go check out another place, but the rest are kind of far away...
Nanami: Oh, perfect timing then! How about we grab something to eat?
Tsukushi: Yeah, let’s do that! ...Oh, right! I saw a place earlier that looked really interesting.
Nanami: Oh, you mean that café you were staring at on the way here? The one with the cute black-cat sign outside?
Tsukushi: Y-you noticed...! Were you interested in it too?
Nanami: Yeah, I thought it had a really nice vibe.
Tsukushi: It looked like the kind of place I’d feel shy going to alone... but if you’re up for it, want to go together?
Nanami: Of course! Let’s go, let’s go~.
Tsukushi: Great! Then let’s have lunch there!
Nanami: Yay~.
(Black cut)
(Background change)

[Café]

Food Cart Clerk: Thank you for waiting. Here’s your omelet rice.
Nanami: Thank you!
Tsukushi: Wow, your omelet rice looks so good, Nanami-chan...!
Nanami: I decided on this one the moment I saw the menu outside~.
Nanami: But your napolitan looks really good too. It’s got that nice old-fashioned feel.
Tsukushi: Right? It was listed as a house recommendation, so I couldn’t resist!
Nanami & Tsukushi: Then... let’s eat!
Nanami: Mmm, mmm... wow~! This is even better than I expected...!
Nanami: The eggs are so soft and creamy, and the buttered rice inside is amazing~.
Tsukushi: My napolitan’s super good too! The way the ketchup’s caramelized gives it this perfect aroma!
Nanami: ...Hey, hey, Tsu-chan?
Tsukushi: Fufu, I was thinking the same thing! Let’s swap a bite!
Nanami: Deal~. Then for you, I’ll give the fluffiest part of the omelet~.
Tsukushi: Really? Thanks! Then for you, I’ll add this big piece of bacon...
(Black cut)
Tsukushi: Ahh~ that was so good~...!
Nanami: Yeah, yeah. Next time, I think I’ll get the napolitan.
Tsukushi: And I was just thinking I’d get the omelet rice next time!
Tsukushi: Oh...
Nanami: Hm? What are you looking at, Tsu-chan~?
Tsukushi: This menu stand. Look.
Nanami: Wow~, pancakes, huh. Ah, they’re shaped like bears, so cute~. If you’re interested, why not order one?
Tsukushi: Yeah, but...
Nanami: ...?
Tsukushi: Wouldn’t it seem kind of childish? It even says here, “Popular with kids.”
Nanami: I don’t think it’s anything to worry about. I’m sure plenty of adults order stuff like that too.
Tsukushi: R-really...?
Nanami: And if you’re still embarrassed, then I, Hiromachi Nanami, solemnly swear to keep today’s secret between us~.
Tsukushi: ...Fufu, what’s that supposed to mean? Okay then, I’ll go for it!
Nanami: Oh, then I’ll get this cream soda~. How about a trade? One spoon of ice cream for one bite of pancake?
Tsukushi: Deal! Excuse me~!

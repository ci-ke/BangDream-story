event300-02 Chapter 1: Maya-sensei’s Quiz Class

Pastel＊Palettes is set to appear on a hit quiz show, so they begin intense training centered on Maya.

(Character: Aya Maruyama, Hina Hikawa, Chisato Shirasagi, Maya Yamato, Eve Wakamiya)

[Talent Agency]

Aya: I can’t believe we get to appear on “Shine☆Quiz Star!” It feels like a dream~!
Hina: The staff seemed super excited too, but... is it really that big of a deal?
Maya: It totally is! It’s a nationally broadcast show that trends almost every week!
Maya: The host is super famous, and the regulars are all stars from different fields.
Hina: Wow, you know a lot about this stuff, Maya-chan. But why did they invite us to such a popular show?
Maya: They always reserve one slot for a rookie team to keep the show from getting stale.
Chisato: Yeah. Even so, it’s a huge opportunity.
Eve: It really is a chance for tons of people to see us!
Aya: Yeah! This is our big shot to make a name for Pastel＊Palettes—let’s give it everything we’ve got!
Hina: But Aya-chan, you’re kinda bad at quizzes, aren’t you?
Aya: Guh...
Chisato: We’ve done little quiz corners before, like on “All Fun and Games,” but this is everyone’s first time on a real quiz show.
Maya: That’s definitely something to be concerned about.
Chisato: So I was thinking—how about we dedicate part of our lesson time for a while to strengthening our skills for the quiz show?
Aya: Nice idea, Chisato-chan!
Maya: In that case, I have a friend at university who’s in the quiz club, so I might be able to act as an instructor.
Hina: Ooh, Maya-chan, you’re good at studying. A quiz training session actually sounds kinda fun!
(Black cut)
(Background change)

[The Next Day]

[Rehearsal Studio]

Maya: When it comes to quizzes, the highlight is definitely the buzzer round. “Shine☆Quiz Star!” also starts with one.
Maya: In these buzzer rounds, quiz pros often buzz in before the full question is even read. Do you know how that’s possible?
Aya: Hmm... maybe they just know a lot of stuff?
Eve: Or they have super fast reflexes?
Maya: Both good guesses.
Maya: I was surprised to learn while studying quizzes that there are certain patterns the questions tend to follow.
Hina: Patterns? Like what?
Maya: Let me give you an example.
Maya: “This British female author, featured on the £10 banknote issued since 2016, is known for works such as ‘Pride and Prejudice.’”
Chisato: The answer is... Jane Austen?
Maya: As expected from Chisato-san—correct!
Chisato: I actually just learned about her in class recently.
Maya: Then you probably figured it out from the second half of the question.
Maya: But if you look closely, just the part about “the £10 note issued since 2016” already narrows it down to one answer.
Hina: So if you realize it there, it’s okay to hit the buzzer?
Maya: Exactly. That’s what we call a pattern where the clue that confirms the answer comes early in the question. Now, how about this one?
Maya: “This French artist, known as a leading figure of the Impressionist movement, created the ‘Water Lilies’ series.”
Eve: Yes! Claude Monet!
Maya: Wow, answering with the full name—impressive. That’s correct.
Maya: As you’ve probably noticed, the first half of this question doesn’t let you pin down the answer yet. That’s because there are other artists who are also key figures of Impressionism.
Chisato: True... in this question, the first half is really just the intro, huh?
Maya: Exactly! This is an example where the key clue comes at the end.
Maya: What’s important is being aware of where in the question the answer becomes clear. That makes it easier to decide when to buzz in.
Maya: If you’ve already narrowed it down at “Impressionist painter,” then when the clue hits, you can buzz in right away.
Aya: Sigh~... I never thought about quiz questions like that before.
Maya: These are just some of the basics, but it's definitely worth keeping in mind.
Chisato: Even if you know the answer, if you’re too slow to buzz in, you can’t use that knowledge.
Chisato: It might be hard to put into practice right away, but just knowing there are techniques like this makes a big difference.
Hina: I always thought quizzes were just about answering stuff you know, but I guess not!
Maya: Yeah. Same here—before I studied, I thought it was just a memory game. But buzzer rounds are packed with strategy.
Eve: Striking at the perfect moment... It’s like drawing your sword in a samurai duel!
Chisato: If we can pull it off, it’ll make for a great moment on the show, too. Let’s definitely go for those buzzer chances.
Aya: Ugh, I wonder if I can do it...
Maya: You’ll be fine. Even wrong answers are part of the fun, and that show’s penalties aren’t too harsh.
Hina: Ahaha, so basically you’re saying Aya-chan’s gonna mess up?
Maya: Th-that’s not what I meant! I just think having the courage to try is what really matters...
Chisato: Of course, just blurting things out isn’t ideal—but being too afraid to speak up is even worse.
Aya: Y-you’re right! Alright, Maya-chan-sensei, keep guiding us!
Maya: Will do! Now, let’s move on to another common quiz format: the “3-para” question type.
Maya: (Fufu, preparing together like this is actually pretty fun. I’m sure everyone will do great during the real show...!)

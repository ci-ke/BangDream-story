event259-02 Chapter 1: Roadblock

Mashiro, after hearing the students' opposition to the campaign, decides to listen to their thoughts—

(Character: Mashiro Kurata, Toko Kirigaya, Nanami Hiromachi, Tsukushi Futaba, Rui Yashio)

[In front of the Student Council Office Tsukinomori Girls' Academy]

Toko: What? You can’t sign it? Why not?
Tsukinomori Student C: Well... isn’t it obvious? Because the director is scary...
Mashiro: Scary...? Oh, you mean you’re afraid something might happen if you agree to sign?
Nanami: Shiro-chan, what do you mean...?
Mashiro: I think... everyone’s scared that the director might find out they signed.
Mashiro: They’re worried something bad will happen if they go against her views...
Tsukinomori Student A: Yeah... that’s exactly it.
Toko: No way! She’d never do something like that!
Rui: I agree. The director may be strict, but she’s a fair person.
Rui: Even if a student does something that goes against her wishes, as long as it follows the rules, she’ll respect it.
Mashiro: Even so... you can’t help but think, ‘What if?’
Mashiro: Even if you know it’s fine, there’s always that ‘What if something happens...?’
Mashiro: I know how that feels too... so I really understand where you’re coming from.
Tsukinomori Student B: ...Hey, are you guys planning to keep collecting signatures?
Tsukushi: Y-Yeah, we are... because we want the Tsukinomori Music Festival to happen. Don’t you?
Tsukinomori Student C: I get how you feel, but... everyone’s really anxious. A lot of people besides us are already planning to return their signature sheets.
Tsukinomori Student B: If this causes the rules to get even stricter... that would be awful. Please, just consider how this might end up causing trouble for everyone.
Mashiro: ――――
Tsukinomori Student A: We don’t want to make it any harder to live our school lives. Well then...
Tsukushi: Trouble... Is that really what everyone thinks?
Toko: That makes no sense! Of course, things happen when you try to do something! But if you’re too scared to take action, then nothing’s ever going to change!
Rui: Not everyone can think that way, you know. Anyway, we need to come up with some kind of solution.
Rui: People are scared that by supporting the signature campaign, they might end up on the director’s radar.
Rui: If we can’t address that fear, the Tsukinomori Music Festival won’t be able to happen.
Mashiro: (...I thought everyone wanted the Tsukinomori Music Festival to happen. But...)
Mashiro: (Does it really make sense to do something no one wants...?)
Tsukushi: (Mashiro-chan...)
(Black cut)
(Background change)

[After School]

[Tsukinomori Girls' Academy Garden]

Teacher: Kurata-san, thanks for your hard work as today’s class rep. Take care on your way home.
Mashiro: Yes... excuse me.
Chairwoman: Good day, Kurata-san.
Mashiro: C-Chairwoman-sensei... G-Good day...
Chairwoman: You’re not with Yashio-san and the others?
Mashiro: I was today’s class rep, so I’m by myself... Um... excuse me...
Chairwoman: Wait a moment. Why are you holding the signature campaign?
Chairwoman: I have no ulterior motives. It’s just a genuine question.
Mashiro: Well... I wanted to bring energy back to Tsukinomori and everyone here.
Mashiro: Even if just for one day, I wanted the school to feel like it used to...
Chairwoman: I see... So it’s a temporary effort, without consideration for the future.
Chairwoman: ... Let me share a bit of my own story. I believe I mentioned that I wasn’t originally a student of Tsukinomori.
Mashiro: Y-Yes...
Chairwoman: So, why did I transfer to Tsukinomori? The reason is—
Chairwoman: I lacked confidence and couldn’t take the first step forward. I wanted to change that about myself.
Mashiro: Wait... you lacked confidence? You, Chairwoman-sensei?
Chairwoman: Surprised?
Mashiro: Very... I’ve always thought of you as someone brimming with confidence.
Mashiro: So, my reason was the same as yours...
Chairwoman: When I first transferred, it was a difficult time.
Chairwoman: The other students were incredibly talented, on a completely different level from other schools. And the solemn traditions and strict rules that Tsukinomori values...
Chairwoman: Being in that environment, I came to realize how small I really was.
Mashiro: I understand... I felt the same way...
Chairwoman: But even so, I wanted to change myself. And as I poured my heart into living my school life...
Chairwoman: Before I knew it, my timid self was gone. Every new thing I accomplished filled my heart with pride.
Chairwoman: The confidence I gained from overcoming these strict rules gave me a sense of radiance and pointed me toward the future I should strive for.
Mashiro: ...Your radiance will light the way...
Chairwoman: Kurata-san, here’s my advice to you. I strongly suggest you stop the signature campaign immediately.
Chairwoman: Wanting to bring energy back to Tsukinomori is a commendable goal. However, that alone will not have any lasting impact on the future.
Chairwoman: What you should focus on now is upholding the solemn rules and embracing the pride of Tsukinomori’s spirit.
Chairwoman: It is from that pride that your true radiance will emerge, guiding you toward the future you are meant to pursue.
(White cut)
(Background change)
Mashiro: (No one at Tsukinomori wanted this... And the chairwoman said it was meaningless...)
Mashiro: (Then... is there even a need to hold the Tsukinomori Music Festival?)
(White cut)

(Memory cut-in)

(Background change)
Mashiro: That's why... I want to give back to Tsukinomori.
Mashiro: For the Tsukinomori Music Festival, I want to put on a concert that can cheer up the depressed Tsukinomori...!
(Black cut)

(Memory cut-out)

(Background change)
Mashiro: (Why did I... Why did I even think something like that back then...?)
(Black cut)

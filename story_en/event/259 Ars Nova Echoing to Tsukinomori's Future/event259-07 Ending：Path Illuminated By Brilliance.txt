event259-07 Ending: Path Illuminated By Brilliance

After the Tsukinomori Music Festival, Morfonica thinks about the future ahead—

(Character: Mashiro Kurata, Toko Kirigaya, Nanami Hiromachi, Tsukushi Futaba, Rui Yashio)

[Tsukinomori Music Festival Backstage]

Tsukushi & Toko: Uuuuugh, that was so much funーーー!!
Toko: That was insane! I can’t even put it into words, but seriously... it was just crazy awesome!!
Tsukushi: I get it, I get it! It felt so amazing, didn’t it? I couldn’t help but think, ‘I want to stay surrounded by this sound forever!’
Nanami: Haha, you two are in high spirits~. Yeah, but... I feel the same way.
Rui: I’m so glad we managed to perform the final song without any problems.
Mashiro: Yeah... it was a close call, though, since we only decided to make that song at the very last minute.
Tsukushi: I was so shocked when Toko-chan suddenly said, ‘We’re gonna perform with the other participants!’
Toko: Well, I mean, they begged us to play with them no matter what. When that happens, we’ve got no choice but to go for it!
Toko: But hey, it turned out to be an amazing moment! Like I always say, I only ever make the best calls~.
Mashiro: Haha... But yeah, I’m glad we got to play with everyone at Tsukinomori.
Mashiro: When I was singing that song, I saw a scene where a shining path stretched into the blue sky.
Mashiro: This time, I could only walk partway down that path, so if I could, I’d love to find out what lies further ahead, even just one more time.
Toko: Hmmm... Then let’s do it again next year! Let’s all play that song together.
Mashiro: Eh? But... isn’t it a bit selfish for us to just decide that on our own, when we’re playing it with everyone else...?
Toko: Why not? That song’s already a classic for the Tsukinomori Music Festival, isn’t it?
Nanami: Haha, it’s turned into a staple now, huh~.
Tsukushi: Geez, you’re always deciding things on your own.
Rui: Still, I agree with playing it again. We didn’t have enough time for proper rehearsals this year.
Rui: I’m satisfied with the performance itself. But there were definitely spots where we could still improve.
Rui: If we get to do it again next year, I’d want to aim for an even more polished performance.
Tsukushi: Wow, thinking about improvements right after it’s over... That’s so like Rui-san.
Rui: That’s just my nature. Now then, let’s go change. The closing ceremony is about to start.
(Black cut)
(Background change)

[Tsukinomori Music Festival Closing Ceremony]

Rui: Good work, everyone. I’m delighted that we were able to complete this year’s Tsukinomori Music Festival.
Toko: That was seriously the bestーーーー!
Rui: ...Please maintain silence. Now, to conclude, we’ll hear some closing remarks from the Chairwoman.
Chairwoman: First of all, well done, everyone. I had the honor of witnessing your Tsukinomori Music Festival.
Chairwoman: Honestly, I couldn’t say whether this year’s Tsukinomori Music Festival was good or bad.
Chairwoman: As I’ve mentioned before, it was completely different from the Tsukinomori Music Festival I’ve known.
Tsukushi: Wait... Is she saying it wasn’t fun?
Nanami: Who knows... But I don’t think that’s what she meant...
Chairwoman: However— The way all of you carried out the Tsukinomori Music Festival with conviction... I thought it was truly beautiful.
Mashiro: Chairwoman-sensei...
Chairwoman: Your final performance was also exceptional. Your feelings of cherishing Tsukinomori came through clearly.
Chairwoman: I suppose I had been clinging to the old Tsukinomori. Because I treasured that scenery, I couldn’t bring myself to acknowledge the present...
Chairwoman: But, even if the form of Tsukinomori changes, the spirit of Tsukinomori continues to be passed down—that, I’ve come to understand.
Chairwoman: That’s why, from now on, I want to work together with all of you, to explore a Tsukinomori where you can shine the brightest.
Chairwoman: I’ll provide more details at the next student assembly. That’s all from me.
(Black cut)
(Background change)
Nanami: Something incredible just happened...
Toko: Totally. I never expected the chairwoman to say something like that.
Tsukushi: But now we're supposed to find out how we can shine the brightest... What are we supposed to do?
Chairwoman: First, I think we should review the school rules.
Mashiro: Oh... Chairwoman-sensei.
Toko: Review the school rules... seriously?! Does that mean we can use our phones at school?!
Chairwoman: I don't know yet. But if it's something that benefits you, then the rules might be revised.
Toko: Totally! It will! If we can use our phones, um... our grades will improve a lot!
Nanami: Toko-chan, even for you, that’s a bit of a stretch...
Rui: Chairwoman-sensei, I don’t think we need to change all the rules to match the students.
Rui: There’s nothing wrong with prioritizing discipline. The student council will help out too, so let’s work together to review the rules.
Toko: What the heck!? Rui, stop saying stuff like that!
Tsukushi: I’m with Rui on this one. Yeah, being too strict is a pain, but having a proper, disciplined school life isn’t a bad thing either.
Toko: Ehhhhh!? Nanami, say something already!
Nanami: Come on, Toko-chan, haven’t there been some good things thanks to those strict rules?
Nanami: Like, your grandma was super happy, right?
Tsukushi: Oh yeah, I know that one! After writing that reflection essay, she said you started knocking before entering rooms, and she praised you for it!
Nanami: Yup, that’s all thanks to the chairwoman being strict with you~.
Toko: Well, yeah... but my grandma got all excited and was like, ‘Let’s celebrate!’ and made a ton of inari sushi...
Mashiro: I... I’ve changed too, thanks to the chairwoman.
Chairwoman: Kurata-san...
Mashiro: Back then, even if something happened at Tsukinomori, I thought, as an outsider, I shouldn’t meddle or say anything.
Mashiro: But when you said, ‘Become a Tsukinomori student’—those words, they made me seriously start thinking about Tsukinomori.
Mashiro: For Tsukinomori's sake, I started to face things head-on, thinking of them as my own problems.
Mashiro: That’s why, Chairwoman-sensei—
Mashiro: I am a Tsukinomori student.
Chairwoman: ......I see.
Chairwoman: Then, I think we should create a place to gather students’ voices. There, I’d like to hear everyone’s opinions.
Chairwoman: Combining my thoughts with all of your ideas... we can work together to make Tsukinomori even better.
Mashiro: Got it!
(Black cut)
(Background change)
Toko: Ughhhh, seriously?! We almost had a chance to use our phones, and now this...
Nanami: Toko-chan, cheer up! It’s not like they’ve decided we can’t use them yet~.
Nanami: If anything, if you properly speak up and share your thoughts, we might actually get permission to use them!
Rui: Yes, exactly. If it’s a reasonable argument, it might even get considered when we're reviewing the rules.
Toko: For real?! Okay, I’m gonna write a ton of suggestions! Rui, Nanami, help me out!
Mashiro: ............
Tsukushi: Mashiro-chan, what are you looking at?
Mashiro: That flower bed over there... there’s a butterfly flying around. It’s so pretty.
Tsukushi: Oh, I see. ...What’s going to happen to Tsukinomori from here on, I wonder?
Mashiro: Hmm, I’m not sure... but I hope we can figure it out together with the chairwoman.
Mashiro: As one of Tsukinomori's students, I want to make it better.
Tsukushi: Yeah, I think so too.
Mashiro: Tsukushi-chan—
Mashiro: We’ll definitely make this an amazing school. Our new Tsukinomori.

event257-07 Ending: Our Dream That Day

As Pastel✽Palettes trains for their next goal, during a break, they stumble upon an intriguing online article...

(Character: Aya Maruyama, Hina Hikawa, Chisato Shirasagi, Maya Yamato, Eve Wakamiya)

[A few days later]

[At the studio]

Nao: Alright, that looks good. Let’s take a 10-minute break!
Pastel＊Palettes: Got it!
Nao: I’m stepping out for a bit, but make sure to actually rest during the break, okay?
Hina: Whew~ That was a good sweat! The performance just now was perfectly in sync, and things have been feeling really great lately, don’t you think?
Eve: Absolutely! We’re all fired up and ready for the A-block finals!
Maya: Hehe, Eve-san’s keyboard playing was so bouncy and fun!
Aya: Oh, guys, look! There’s a new article about Pastel*Palettes online!
Hina: Really? What’s it say? ‘The dark horse of TIS: The rising idol band Pastel*Palettes is one to watch!’ Ooh, sounds interesting!
Maya: Hmm, looks like they used the same profile the agency put together.
Hina: Boooring! If they’d interviewed me, I could’ve spilled some juicy secrets about Aya-chan!
Aya: Huh?! What kind of secrets?!
Hina: What? You want me to say it here?
Chisato: Hina-chan, stop teasing so much. Anyway, Aya-chan, what does the rest of the article say?
Aya: Um... it looks like the next part is about TIS. ‘A dark past for Pastel*Palettes’ producer...?’
Aya: Huh...?
Online News: ‘The producer for Pastel＊Palettes is Nao Higashinakano, a former member of the popular idol group Marmalade.’
Online News: ‘Marmalade’s sudden disbandment a few years ago is still fresh in people’s minds. While officially described as amicable, the truth behind it was far more tragic.’
Online News: ‘The real reason was due to the mental exhaustion of Miharu, one of the members.’
Online News: ‘Overflowing with charisma, Miharu felt crushed by the pressures of the entertainment industry. Exploited and worn down by her agency, she was forced to retire completely shattered.’
Aya: ‘We hope Pastel＊Palettes doesn’t meet the same fate...’
Aya: No way...
Eve: N-No way! This is all lies! It’s got to be lies!
Maya: Aya-san, don’t let it bother you.
Chisato: That’s right. When you’re in the spotlight, rumors like this are bound to appear. There’s no need to pay attention to baseless gossip.
Aya: Y-Yeah, you’re right. There’s no way something like that happened with Marmalade, right?
Aya: Their final concert... Nao-san and the others, they were shining so brightly until the very end... Oh.
(White cut)

(Memory cut-in)

Nao: She loved idols, she loved the idol industry, she loved her fans, and she was a positive girl who wanted to live up to everyone's expectations.
Nao: But, it was too hard to meet the expectations of the industry and her fans… And it broke her heart. She was so exhausted, both physically and mentally, that she couldn't be an idol anymore.
(White cut)

(Memory cut-out)

Aya: ............
Chisato: Aya-chan...?
Aya: Hey, remember when Nao-san mentioned ‘an idol acquaintance’ in a story a while back...?
Aya: Doesn’t it sound a lot like what’s written in this article...?
Maya: ...Oh.
Aya: Could it be... that idol she mentioned... was Miharu-san...?
Eve: N-No way! That’s impossible! It couldn’t be...!
Hina: You know, when I was gathering materials at the idol research club the other day, I stumbled upon an old magazine.
Hina: It had almost the exact same story as this article.
Aya, Maya & Eve: ！！
Hina: When I found it, I figured it wasn’t worth bringing up, so I just stayed quiet about it.
Aya: W-What... So this article... does that mean it’s—
Hina: But, back then, it didn’t stir up much attention, right? That probably means no one believed it. So maybe it’s just not credible after all.
Chisato: When you put it like that... It really does seem like it could just be fabricated. If it were true, surely it would’ve become a bigger deal in the industry.
Eve: Y-Yeah, exactly. This article must be completely made up—
Nao: I’m back! Alright, let’s get back to practice— Wait, why’s everyone looking so serious all of a sudden?
Aya: ............
Nao: Why’s everyone glued to their phones? Let me see... oh.
Nao: ...So, you found out, huh.
Aya: Nao-san...
Nao: We tried reaching out to stop it, but in the end, it got published anyway, by force.
Maya: Wait, Nao-san, does this have something to do with that email...?
Nao: Yeah. I’m really sorry for making you all upset during such an important time.
Nao: It’s kind of a long story, but would you let me explain it myself?
Nao: This goes back to when I first joined the talent agency...
(Black cut)
(Background change)
Nao: Honestly, I joined the agency with such a casual attitude— like, ‘Hey, this might be fun!’ And that’s where I met Ayumi and Miharu, and we became idols together.
Nao: About six months after we formed, we went to see this one idol group’s live performance. That moment... that’s when everything changed.
(White cut)
(Background change)
Miharu: Wow... Idols are amazing. Truly amazing...!
Miharu: Hey, let’s become the legends of idols!
Nao: Huh? Legends?
Miharu: Yeah, let’s aim to be that incredible. We can do it—together. Let’s keep chasing this dream forever!
Ayumi: Yeah, I want to! Let’s do it, all of us—right, Nao-chan?
Nao: Man, you two, always going over the top... But hey, maybe that kind of thing isn’t so bad after all.
(White cut)
(Background change)
Nao: We all dreamed of becoming idols. And from that moment on, everything was so much fun. Work, lessons... none of it felt like a chore.
Nao: Finally, we climbed all the way to the Budokan stage. But from there... that’s where the end began for us.
Maya: Huh? Why? You achieved your dream of performing at the Budokan, didn’t you?
Nao: That’s exactly why. The Budokan was a dream we shared with our fans for years... But once we achieved that dream... we couldn’t find the next one.
Eve: The next one?
Nao: Yeah, the next dream. Something even bigger, even more incredible, even brighter. We felt like we had to show that to our fans. But...
Nao: That dream became pressure instead of inspiration. The fans’ expectations, their support, they started to weigh on us. Our activities became awkward, strained...
Nao: And Miharu... she started saying things like, ‘This isn’t good enough. This isn’t what people expect of us. We can’t inspire anyone like this,’ with this completely lifeless look in her eyes.
Nao: She loved being an idol so much, but... she was completely crushed. I kept wondering what we could do... what we should do...
Nao: It was Ayumi who spoke up first.
Nao: ‘Miharu-chan, Nao-chan, let’s give it our all one last time. Let’s create a live performance where everyone can say, that was the best ever—and then, let’s bring it to an end.’
Nao: And so, we had that final concert... the one that became our farewell.
Aya: ...I can’t believe it. Marmalade... you were all shining so brightly...
Maya: Um, so... what happened to Miharu-san after that?
Nao: After the farewell concert, she said she was leaving the entertainment industry and going back to her hometown.
Nao: But since then, not once has she reached out. I don’t know where she is now, or what she’s doing...
Maya: ............
Nao: The weight of expectations... from being an idol, and from all the people around idols... it broke her. And that... that’s what triggered the end for us.
Pastel＊Palettes: ............
Eve: So that’s why Nao-san always says, ‘Don’t let expectations weigh you down.’...
Aya: ............
(Black cut)
(Background change)

event267-03 Chapter 2: Words from the Fans

Not all fan messages leave one happy. As Aya struggles with this, Chisato shares some heartfelt words...

(Character: Aya Maruyama, Hina Hikawa, Chisato Shirasagi, Maya Yamato, Eve Wakamiya)

[Station Entrance]

Maya: Just like the forecast said, it’s started raining.
Hina: Yeah. I’m glad I listened to my sister and brought a folding umbrella.
Eve: Well then, good work today. I’ll see you all tomorrow.
Chisato: Yes, see you tomorrow.
Aya: Bye, everyone.
Aya: Phew...
Chisato: ...Aya-chan, are you heading straight home now?
Aya: Yeah... Why do you ask, Chisato-chan?
Chisato: Mind taking a bit of a detour with me? The rain’s a bit of a pain, but I’d like to talk a little more.
Aya: Chisato-chan... Sure, let’s walk home together.
Aya: The rain just isn't letting up, huh.
Chisato: No, it isn’t. I hope it clears up tomorrow...
Aya: ...Hey, Chisato-chan. What does it really mean to live up to fans’ expectations?
Aya: I’ve definitely tried too hard before and ended up spinning my wheels... But I’ve never found it painful or felt like it was a burden...
Aya: I just really love the cheers from our fans. They give me so much energy, and no matter how down I am, I feel this fire light up inside me...!
Chisato: Yes. I think that’s perfectly fine, Aya-chan.
Aya: Thank you, Chisato-chan... But even if that’s how I feel, I’ve been wondering if that makes it okay to just ignore Miharu-san’s feelings and what Marmalade’s going through...
Aya: But to me, our fans’ expectations are truly precious... They give me so much courage... But still...
Aya: Ugh, I keep saying “but” and going in circles... Sorry...
Chisato: Aya-chan...
Chisato: Hey, Aya-chan. I said before that I’ve felt the pressure of fans’ expectations... But I also understand where they’re coming from.
Chisato: Because I’m a fan of Aya Maruyama.
Aya: ...
Aya: Wha!? Eeeehhhhhhh~~!?
Chisato: Oh? I thought it was obvious by now.
Aya: That’s the first I’ve heard of it~!
Chisato: Fufufu.
Chisato: When I was a child actress, all I ever thought about was success... But you gave me a reason to dream.
Chisato: No matter what happens, you never give up. You’re so straightforward it’s almost reckless... That’s the kind of Aya Maruyama I’m a fan of.
Aya: Chisato-chan...
Chisato: Fufu. Since the timing’s perfect, I’ll say this as your fan too.
Aya: Eeh, that’s making me nervous...
Chisato: Aya-chan.
Aya: Y-Yes...!
Chisato: Always stay true to your dreams.
Aya: ...!
Chisato: Fufu, saying it out loud like this is kind of embarrassing.
Aya: Uuu... Thank you, Chisato-chan~...!
Aya: Hearing that from you makes me so happy...! It doesn’t feel like a burden at all.
Aya: I’ll treasure those words forever.
Chisato: Hey, Aya-chan. Tomorrow when we get to the office, why don’t we all read through the fan letters together?
Aya: Huh...?
Chisato: Let’s take a moment to really think about what kinds of messages our fans send us, and how they make us feel... Maybe we could try putting those feelings into words again.
Chisato: We might discover something important in the process.
Aya: Chisato-chan...
Aya: Yeah, you’re right...!
Aya: Then I guess we should ask everyone to come a bit earlier. Let’s see... phone, phone...
Chisato: Aya-chan, calm down. I’ll hold the umbrella.
Aya: Thanks, Chisato-chan.
Aya: ...Hey, Chisato-chan.
Chisato: What is it?
(White cut)
(Background change)
Aya: You’re really important to me too.
Aya: You’ve always supported me, worked hard alongside me, and helped me notice the things that really matter—thank you so much!
Chisato: Aya-chan...
Chisato: You’re very welcome.
(White cut)
(Background change)

<A Small Treasure>

(Character: Tsugumi Hazawa, Sayo Hikawa)

[Hazawa Coffee]

Tsugumi: All right! Let's look over the novel again to work out a chocolate roll cake recipe!
Sayo: Okay, I will do my best.
Sayo: Our first major clue... is this page covering how the two initially meet. It says the roll cake has a glistening cocoa brown surface.
Tsugumi: Glistening... That could mean it's glazed.
Sayo: Glazed...?
Tsugumi: Yes. Spreading melted gelatin on the sponge cake would give the surface a nice finish.
Tsugumi: And since the color is supposed to be cocoa brown, maybe we should add some cocoa powder to the mix.
Sayo: I see, understood. Now our next clue is this one... It says the sponge cake is soft and moist...
Tsugumi: That suggests it may contain a lot of milk.
Sayo: Additionally... there is supposed to be a pleasant aroma.
Tsugumi: I wonder where that could come from... Maybe a touch of alcohol, and since it's a chocolate roll cake, rum might go well with it...
Sayo: Let us add that to the recipe as well, then.
Sayo: ...
Tsugumi: What is it?
Sayo: According to this description, the walnuts are golden brown...
Tsugumi: Ah, I remember that! The main character says she especially likes the walnuts.
Sayo: Yes... What I find peculiar is that walnuts are normally a lighter color and more like a faint shade of beige...
Tsugumi: True... But the author went out of their way to describe them as golden brown, so there has to be a reason, right?
Sayo: Perhaps there is a particular kind of golden-brown walnut that I am simply unaware of...
Tsugumi: ... Ah! Or they might be candied...!
Sayo: Candied...?
Tsugumi: Yes, simmered in sugar and water until they become caramel brown.
Tsugumi: You see it done with other fruits and nuts, and it helps make them more savory and sweet. The color also becomes a nice golden brown.
Sayo: Interesting. That would certainly match the part of the novel that states they have an unforgettable gentle sweetness.
Sayo: I think we can safely assume that the walnuts are candied.
Tsugumi: Okay, then let's jot this down and add it to the recipe too!
Tsugumi: Fufu, isn't it so exciting to work together to figure out how to make the roll cake? Something we only imagined based on words is going to become a reality!
Sayo: Yes. And I have to say, I am impressed with how accurately you have managed to determine the steps involved using only the descriptions in the novel...
Sayo: When it comes to making snacks, you still remain my teacher, just like back then.
Tsugumi: N-no, no! I'm no teacher...!
Sayo: You were also the one who told me of the existence of a model for the roll cake and suggested that we attempt to make it.
Sayo: That positivity and ability to act... are just a few of the many things I still must learn from you.
Tsugumi: I-I think you're putting me on a bit of a pedestal...!
Tsugumi: Um, anyway, doesn't going over this novel remind you of our trip together?
Sayo: It does... And I feel I am even more fond of this book than before.
Sayo: Take this page mentioning the park with flowers from every season. I can picture it even more clearly now...
Sayo: ... That reminds me... We never went to the stationery store that shows up after the park scene...
Tsugumi: Oh, you're right. The scene there where they buy the same pen is such a nice one too.
Sayo: I agree. It is how they slowly start accumulating numerous small yet precious treasures...
Tsugumi: Yes... There are so many wonderful scenes...
Tsugumi: ... Oh, I know. What do you think about going back again together?
Tsugumi: There are still plenty of places we didn't get to visit, and we might even discover new things about the ones we've already been to!
Sayo: ... I would like that very much, actually.
Tsugumi: Great! I can't wait!
Tsugumi: When we do go back, I hope the café will be open. Ah, I also want to go to the bus stop.
Sayo: You must be referring to the one in the scene where they have a leisurely chat during the rain.
Tsugumi: That's the one! I love the part where they're waiting for the bus and talking about what the rain sounds like.
Sayo: The scene is one of my favorites as well.
Tsugumi: Fufu... Just thinking about being able to go back makes me so excited.
Sayo: Fufu, perhaps we should complete this recipe first, though. We still need to identify the cream.
Tsugumi: Oops, right! Okay, let's try to come up with a recipe that everyone will like!
Sayo: Yes.

<To Someday Become Dependable>

(Character: Tsugumi Hazawa)

[Hazawa Coffee]

Tsugumi: Welcome to Hazawa Coffee! Ah, Marina-san, {{userName}}-san! Hello!
Marina: Hi, Tsugumi-chan. Hope you don't mind us swinging by for a break after our shopping run.
Tsugumi: Not at all. Thank you for coming! Ah, could you hold on a little?
Tsugumi: Thank you for waiting. We made some chocolate roll cake for Valentine's Day, and I'd like you to have some if you'd like!
Marina: Wow, are you sure it's okay for us to have this?
Tsugumi: Of course! It's my way of thanking you for everything you always do!
Marina: Thanks! Even the roll cake's clear plastic box and ribbon are cute.
Tsugumi: Ehehe... That's a little something we also all worked on when we baked the roll cakes.
Marina: It looks absolutely delicious~. I can't believe you made something like this. It must've been a lot of work.
Tsugumi: No, it was actually really fun! You see, this roll cake appears in a book I like, and Sayo-san and I went over the story together to come up with the recipe for it.
Marina: Ah, that reminds me. The other day, Sayo-chan mentioned how the two of you went to where the story takes place.
Tsugumi: Yes, we did, and it was such a magical day! I felt like I was inside the novel itself!
Tsugumi: Even just walking around the town was so exciting...
Tsugumi: The idea that we were at the school route or knick-knack shop where certain events happened brought back the feelings I felt when I read about them in the story!
Tsugumi: There was this beautiful park that was filled with flowers, and I could see the scene in the novel unfolding before my eyes...
Tsugumi: ... Ah! I-I'm sorry. I've been the only one talking this whole time...!
Marina: Fufu, you don't need to apologize. Just seeing how happy you are as you talk is making me have a good time.
Marina: Anyway, it sounds like it was a wonderful trip. When Sayo-chan told us about it, it sounded like she had fun too.
Tsugumi: Wow, really?
Marina: Mm-hmm. So, how did it feel to go on a trip with just the two of you?
Tsugumi: It was great! We happened to have the same favorite scenes, and we even went to a drink place that served as the model for the one in the story...
Tsugumi: She also always somehow noticed and suggested doing what I was interested in without me saying anything.
Tsugumi: And even when we did things that weren't part of the plan, she was so nice and considerate towards me...
Marina: Fufu, I see.
Tsugumi: You should've seen how kind she was when we were making roll cakes together with everyone!
Tsugumi: The instructions she gave to Kasumi-chan and the others there were so clear and thorough...
Tsugumi: She even prepared notes so that she'd be able to explain better to the people without much baking experience.
Tsugumi: I really respect how considerate she is towards others and how she's always so willing to put in the effort!
Marina: Yeah, her diligence and kindness are probably part of the big reason why everyone is so willing to depend on her.
Tsugumi: I hope I can someday become someone who's just as dependable and capable of such kindness and consideration...
Tsugumi: Ah, I'm sorry, Marina-san. This is supposed to be your break...!
Marina: Don't worry about it. It was great getting the chance to hear what you have to say.
Tsugumi: Thank you very much. I hope you enjoy your time here!

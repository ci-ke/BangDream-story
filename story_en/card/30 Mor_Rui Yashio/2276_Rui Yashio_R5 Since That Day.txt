Rui: You were looking at your notebook with quite a serious expression. Were you thinking about lyrics for a new song?
Mashiro: Ah... Y-yeah. Though it's not really in a form I could call lyrics yet...
Mashiro: I wanted to write down and put into words what I felt at our last show and how I'm feeling now.
Mashiro: Maybe it could become a phrase I can use for the song you entrusted to me, Rui-san.
Rui: ...I see.
Mashiro: Ever since you gave me that song the other day, I've been constantly thinking about what kind of lyrics would fit...
Mashiro: But it's still difficult for me right now... I can't quite picture us performing that song.
Rui: ...Yes.
Mashiro: But you know, after doing that last show, I feel like I've moved forward a bit...
Mashiro: Like I've taken one step closer to the goal of performing that song... That's how it feels.
Mashiro: What do you think, Rui-san...?
Rui: ...Well. I feel the same way.
Mashiro: Really...?!
Rui: Yes. Though the catalyst was unexpected.
Mashiro: Ah... Could it be that you decided to teach Toko-chan guitar for that song too...?
Rui: ...That was one of the reasons.
Rui: When I was thinking about what would be necessary to eventually perform that song at a show, Kirigaya-san made her request at just the right time.
Mashiro: So that's how it was.
Rui: What kind of preparation would be needed to perform that song, and how much time it would take...
Rui: I was thinking about our future by working backward from Morfonica's current position, but...
Rui: Kirigaya-san's request ended up greatly shortening that estimate.
Mashiro: I see... Does that mean Toko-chan's hard work exceeded your expectations, Rui-san?
Rui: Kirigaya-san herself probably didn't intend it that way, but I learned that there's also an approach driven by personal feelings like that.
Mashiro: Fufu, when Toko-chan wants to do something, she just charges forward with nothing but the feeling that she'll definitely be able to do it.
Rui: That's right. It's a method I couldn't use. ...Not that I'd try to imitate it.
Mashiro: Fufufu...! True, I'd be surprised if Rui-san practiced the way Toko-chan does.
Rui: That would be... quite something.
Mashiro: ...Ah, sorry, I got carried away talking! You said you came to check the locks, right?
Rui: Yes. Will your work take much longer?
Mashiro: Yeah, just a bit more... I want to write down phrases until I reach a good stopping point...
Rui: It's almost time to leave school, and the student council can't really approve personal staying behind without a form...
Mashiro: Y-yeah, you're right...
Rui: ...I'll go check the other classrooms first.
Mashiro: ...! Thank you, Rui-san...! I'll finish up right away...!
Rui: Well then, see you later.
Mashiro: ...Ah, Rui-san!
Rui: What is it?
Mashiro: I'm looking forward to performing that song at a show someday.
Rui: ...Yes, me too.


<Teaching Is Half Learning>

[Shopping Mall]

Rui: Ah...
Marina: Rui-chan! Hello.
Rui: Hello, Tsukishima-san, {{userName}}-san. Thanks for your hard work.
Marina: Thanks for yours too. Are you out shopping, Rui-chan?
Rui: Yes. I needed a book.
Marina: I see. Can I ask what you bought?
Rui: A guitar instruction book.
Marina: A guitar instruction book...?
Rui: The other day, I had the opportunity to teach Kirigaya-san guitar. I studied beforehand for that as well, but...
Rui: In case something similar happens again in the future, I thought I should learn a bit more so I can teach more effectively.
Marina: Ah, I heard about that from Tsukushi-chan and Toko-chan.
Marina: Toko-chan said she was glad you taught her.
Rui: Is that so...? She didn't really seem that way.
Marina: Fufu, maybe she was embarrassed.
Marina: But you're admirable too, Rui-chan. To think about studying properly for the next time you teach someone.
Rui: No, it's only natural. If I'm going to teach someone, I can't say anything incorrect or inefficient.
Marina: I see. Taking it on with a sense of responsibility is definitely important.
Marina: How was it actually teaching her? I have a feeling it might've been tough.
Rui: Well... I won't deny that.
Rui: Even when I thought I was explaining things clearly, there were many times when it didn't get through to Kirigaya-san at all.
Rui: But through teaching Kirigaya-san, I think I was able to encounter ways of thinking and sensibilities different from my own.
Marina: True, Toko-chan feels more like she goes by instinct, and you seem more like the logical type, Rui-chan.
Rui: Yes, and if I approach it understanding that our ways of thinking are different, I can avoid inefficient instruction.
Marina: Those kinds of things might actually be what you notice for the first time when you become the teacher.
Rui: That's right. In that sense, it was a learning experience for me as well.
Rui: Well then, I have practice now, so I should get going. Sorry for keeping you.
Marina: No, it's fine. Good luck with practice.

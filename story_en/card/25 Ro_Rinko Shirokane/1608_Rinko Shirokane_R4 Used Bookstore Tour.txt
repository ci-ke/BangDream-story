1608_Rinko Shirokane_R4 Used Bookstore Tour

Gacha phrase: I hope both of you can find books... that are perfect for you...

<Melody of Books Pt. 1>

(Character: Kanon Matsubara, Chisato Shirasagi, Rinko Shirokane)

[Used Bookstore]

Rinko: Um... We're here... This is the used bookstore... I come to often...
Kanon: Wow~, so this is it... The store looks like it's been around for a long time. It has a lovely atmosphere.
Chisato: I agree. And there are so many shelves of books. With this many to choose from, I think I may find a book that interests me.
Rinko: Yes... This is an old store... It's famous for its large collection... and for also having many rare books...
Chisato: Oh? So it has many rare books... I should've expected as much from a store you like.
Kanon: Yes. I had been curious about what kind of place it'd be ever since you said you had a favorite secondhand bookstore.
Chisato: We tagged along with you while you were shopping, but I hope we're not troubling you.
Rinko: No, not at all... I'm happy... to show you a store I like...
Rinko: Out of... the many used bookstores I go to... I come here especially often...
Chisato: It's hard to believe you frequent so many used bookstores... You must like them very much.
Rinko: Yes... I like the atmosphere in these stores...
Rinko: Being in a quiet store... surrounded by books... is kind of relaxing...
Rinko: Maybe... I like the smell... of places like this...
Kanon: The smell of secondhand bookstores... It's true they have a strange scent. It's almost nostalgic, like the smell of your grandmother's house...
Rinko: It must be... the smell... of old books...
Rinko: I think it's how the sunlight... and humidity... affect the ink and chemicals in the books... that makes this scent...
Rinko: Different chemicals get used too... so I've heard... that every book... has its own smell...
Chisato: I see. I knew this was how books smelled, but I didn't know what caused it.
Kanon: You must know a lot to know that, Rinko-chan.
Rinko: I don't know that much... I was just curious about it when I was little and looked it up...
Kanon: Fufu, so you've liked secondhand bookstores ever since you were a child. You're right, though, this smell might be relaxing.
Rinko: Ah, but... the smell... isn't the only thing I like about these stores...
Rinko: I also like... how at used bookstores... no two books are alike...
Chisato: Each book is different...?
Rinko: Yes... Each has signs of being reread or writing in them... or pages with creases...
Rinko: Which lines... moved a reader when they read that book... Which stories they liked...
Rinko: Each book seems to hold the feelings of its previous owner... I like to imagine those things when I read...
Chisato: Imagining the thoughts of the person who read the book... I haven't read many used books, but that sounds like a nice way to enjoy them.
Kanon: That's a lovely way to read them. Maybe I'll try to do that too...
Kanon: Ah, I know. Would you tell me a book you recommend?
Rinko: A-a book... I recommend...?
Chisato: In that case, may I ask the same of you? Since we're here, I would be happy if you chose a book for me.
Kanon: Yeah, I'd like to read one how you said you do.
Rinko: A book I recommend... W-well...
Rinko: ...
Kanon: ...? Rinko-chan, what's wrong?
Chisato: Y-you look a little troubled... Um, Rinko-chan?
Rinko: I-I'm sorry... There are many books... I would like to recommend... but I'm not sure... which ones to choose...
Rinko: U-um, I'd like to really think about it... so would it be okay... if I had a little more time...?
Kanon: Y-you're thinking about it so seriously...
Chisato: I'm sorry, Rinko-chan. I didn't mean to trouble you that much...
Rinko: N-no... I'm the one who should apologize...
Kanon: So you have so many books you could recommend that it's that hard to choose.
Rinko: I could recommend... books from every genre... Even within the same genre... each book has something unique and wonderful... So it's hard to choose... one...
Chisato: Fufu, you really like books, don't you?
Kanon: You sure do. I knew you liked books, but you still surprised me today.
Rinko: Huh...?
Chisato: Me too. It's amazing that you know so many works you could recommend. That's proof that you've enjoyed many books.
Rinko: R-really...? I've never... thought of it that way...
Rinko: ...
Rinko: U-um... It's hard for me to choose one book now, but...
Chisato & Kanon: ...?
Rinko: On the shelves over there are illustrated and photo books about the sea... Matsubara-san... you might find a book you like there...
Rinko: And... I don't know much about plays... but there are books by theatre directors that even I've heard of... in that corner...
Rinko: Maybe... you'll find a book... you'd like there... Shirasagi-san...
Chisato: ...
Rinko: Um, what's wrong...?
Chisato: Nothing. I was just surprised by how well you know the store.
Kanon: Me too. That's amazing you remember all that. I'm going to look in the corner with books about the sea.
Chisato: Thank you, Rinko-chan. I'll look carefully for a book.
Rinko: Please do... I hope both of you can find books... that are perfect for you...


<Melody of Books Pt. 2>

(Character: Kanon Matsubara, Chisato Shirasagi, Rinko Shirokane)

[Café]

Rinko: So there was a shop like this here... I visit this area often... but I never knew about it...
Kanon: It has a really nice feel to it, doesn't it? I've been telling Chisato-chan over and over that we should come here.
Chisato: It's famous for its delicious handmade cakes and tea.
Rinko: I see... I couldn't come to this kind of shop alone... so it's nice to be here.
Kanon: Fufu, then I'm glad the three of us came today.
Rinko: Me too... I'm looking forward to trying the cake...
Rinko: Ah... By the way... you both bought something at the used bookstore... Did you find a book you liked...?
Kanon: Yes. I found one in the corner you told me to look in.
Kanon: Let's see, I chose this book. It's a story about a jellyfish exploring the sea. The illustrations are really cute.
Chisato: It has so many illustrations of sea life. Fufu, I think that's the perfect book for you.
Kanon: I think so too. What kind of book did you get?
Chisato: I chose this book of plays. I had been looking for it for a very long time. It's out of print and hard to find...
Chisato: I never thought I'd see it at that store.
Rinko: I'm happy... you could find a book... you've spent a long time searching for...
Chisato: It was all thanks to you introducing me to the store. Thank you.
Rinko: No... I think you found it... because you were always looking for it...
Kanon: Fufu, that's great, Chisato-chan. Rinko-chan, didn't you buy a book earlier too?
Rinko: I bought this book... It's about a solitary pianist...
Kanon: A pianist... That's just like you. Why did you choose it?
Rinko: I read the first few pages... and felt that... this writer and I... shared some things in common...
Chisato: Really...? Why did you feel that?
Rinko: Well... maybe it's how they write with reservation... or the way they choose their words... I think they're like me... Someone who's not good at being assertive...
Rinko: I wanted to know... what a person like that hoped to communicate so badly that they'd write a book...
Chisato: So you chose this book... not only based on its content, but also on your impression of the author. That sounds very like you, Rinko-chan.
Rinko: Yes... Being able to enjoy books this way... may also be a reason why I like them...
Rinko: When I can sense a writer's feelings... I feel as if I've communicated with them... That makes me happy...
Kanon: That's such a lovely way to read.
Rinko: Ah...
Kanon: What is it?
Rinko: Ah, well... I thought... that's kind of how I feel... about music...
Kanon: Huh? Music...? What do you mean?
Rinko: When you play together... do you ever feel the emotions of your band members... through the music...?
Rinko: For example, today someone feels happy... or today someone feels sad...
Rinko: I'm not sure how... but I feel like I can understand those things... And I think that... is similar to how I feel about books...
Kanon: Wow... Maybe they are similar when you think about it that way.
Chisato: A link between books and music...
Chisato: Perhaps we haven't noticed it, but there could be similarities between music and the things Kanon and I like as well.
Kanon: Then maybe jellyfish and playing in a band share something similar too.
Chisato: I wonder. But it may be up to you to find out.
Kanon: Thinking about it is a little fun.
Rinko: Yes... I've become very excited for my next band practice...
Chisato: I'm going to read this book to prepare for my next TV show filming.
Kanon: Today has been such a nice day with great books and fun discoveries!
Rinko: Fufu. Yes, it has... I've never enjoyed myself this much visiting used bookstores...

Sayo: Yes, it’s lovely. Well then... let me try it.
Sayo: ...

Tsugumi: Y-yeah... same here...
Sayo: Mmm... It’s delicious. The sweetness is just right, and it melts smoothly in your mouth.
Tsukushi: Wow! Thank you so much!
Tsugumi: Fufu, I think my dad will be really happy. I’m also relieved to have Sayo-san’s seal of approval.
Sayo: Seal of approval...? You’re the one who taught me how to bake—my pastry mentor, remember?
Tsukushi: Eh, pastry mentor...? Tsugumi-senpai is your mentor, Sayo-senpai!?
Tsugumi: Ahaha, I’m not sure I’ve done enough to be called that.
Tsugumi: There was a time when we held a sweets-making class at my family’s shop, and Sayo-san came to learn.
Tsukushi: Oh, I had no idea that ever happened.
Sayo: I was a bit lost that day and probably caused you some trouble.
Tsugumi: Not at all!
Tsugumi: You were so focused and serious— I figured you’d improve really quickly.
Sayo: Thank you. Everything you taught me that day is still a precious lesson to me.
Sayo: Even now, whenever I find myself tensing up too much, I remember your advice about “balance” and try to stay mindful of it.
Sayo: That’s why you really are my mentor when it comes to sweets, Hazawa-san.
Tsugumi: Being called a mentor is a little embarrassing... but it really makes me happy to hear that!
Tsukushi: Fufu, what a lovely story. I had no idea you used to hold baking classes here.
Sayo: I see. In that case, why not consider holding another event like that? I'm sure it would be a big hit.
Tsugumi: Fufu, that could be nice.
Tsugumi: And now that Tsukushi-chan’s here too, we could probably do even more than before!
Tsukushi: Leave it to me! I’ll support you however I can!
Tsugumi: Thanks, Tsukushi-chan.
Tsugumi: Sayo-san, I know you’re super busy, but if we do hold one again, I hope you can come!
Sayo: Of course. I’d look forward to it.

<Grieving Over Another Story>

(Character: Kaoru Seta, Chisato Shirasagi)

[Yotsuba Women's University - Theater Area]

Kaoru: ...Ah, my princess. Do you remember... the memories of that summer day...
Kaoru: No, that’s not quite right... It needs more passion...
Kaoru: Ah, my princess! Do you remember...! Hmm...
Chisato: You're as devoted as ever, Kaoru.
Kaoru: Oh... Is that you, Chisato? For a second, I thought a real princess had appeared.
Chisato: ...Maybe I shouldn't have come after all.
Chisato: I had a bit of free time, so I thought I’d invite you out for tea for a change.
Kaoru: What an honor. An invitation from Chisato herself... I’d be delighted— Or, that’s what I’d like to say, but...
Kaoru: The truth is, I’ve hit a wall with this role. So I’ve been running through rehearsals on my own like this.
Kaoru: If you don’t mind, I’d love to hear your thoughts.
Chisato: Just a little. So? What kind of role is it this time?
Kaoru: I’m playing a prince. He travels to a neighboring kingdom to study abroad and meets a beautiful princess. The two quickly grow close...
Chisato: Sounds like a perfect fit for you.
Kaoru: Ah, I’d like to think so myself. But the trouble starts after that.
Kaoru: When his time abroad ends, the prince has to return home. He promises the princess they’ll meet again, and leaves the kingdom behind.
Kaoru: But the prince wasn’t able to see the princess again until many years had passed. And then...
Kaoru: When they were finally reunited, the princess had shut herself away completely... like she was a different person.
Kaoru: The prince tries everything he can to open her heart. But the princess keeps rejecting him.
Chisato: I think I’ve heard that story before, vaguely.
Chisato: If I recall, even after the prince left, the princess kept longing for him... but eventually, that longing turned into resentment, right?
Kaoru: Exactly... The long years apart made her close her heart.
Chisato: They both grew up and couldn’t stay innocent children forever...
Kaoru: Time can be such a cruel thing. By the way, Chisato... Do you know how this story ends?
Chisato: Hmm... I’m not sure.
Kaoru: The prince realizes it. That the reason the princess shut her heart was because she felt lonely.
Kaoru: He apologizes for leaving her all alone... for not being there when she needed him.
Kaoru: And hearing that, the princess finally smiles again, and their hearts reconnect.
Kaoru: Fufu... Doesn’t it remind you of someone?
Chisato: Wait, are you talking about...
Kaoru: Yes. Doesn’t it sound just like Kokoro and Charlotte-chan?
Chisato: Kanon's told me a bit about them. It really does resemble their story.
Kaoru: Ah, and their story had a happy ending. But the prince I’m playing...
Chisato: Where exactly are you getting stuck in the story?
Kaoru: It’s the final scene, where the prince melts the ice around the princess’s heart. In that moment, he feels guilt for what he did... and his unwavering love...
Kaoru: But... Is that really all there is to it?
Chisato: I see. So it’s the prince’s emotions in that last scene that don’t quite sit right with you.
Kaoru: Exactly. No matter how many times I rehearse, I just can’t get through that part.
Chisato: In that case, why not try thinking about it from a different angle?
Kaoru: A different angle?
Chisato: For example... Well, the story ends there, but what do you think happens to them afterward?
Chisato: Right now, they’ve reconnected... But what if something small causes them to drift apart again?
Kaoru: Chisato... Are you trying to trouble this already troubled prince even more?
Chisato: Oh my, was that a bit too mean?
Kaoru: No... The heart is a fickle thing. Misunderstandings happen all the time.
Kaoru: But this prince and princess will understand each other again and again. They’ll always find a way to hold hands.
Chisato: Oh? And why are you so sure of that?
Kaoru: Because deep down, they both want to reach for each other.
Chisato: And how can you be so sure the princess feels the same?
Kaoru: I can’t... But at the very least, the prince believes she does. That they feel the same way.
Chisato: Fufu, that’s such a you answer. So? Think that helped your acting at all?
Kaoru: Ahh, you really are amazing, Chisato. This is exactly the answer I was looking for.
Chisato: So it’s not just guilt, and not just love. What’s really in the prince’s heart is a deep trust in the princess. That interpretation suits you.
Kaoru: Now that I know that, I’ll have to rethink everything from the opening scene onward.
Kaoru: Thank you, Chisato. Looks like I’ll make it in time for opening night after all.
Chisato: I didn’t do anything worth thanking me for. So, when’s the performance?
Kaoru: The day after tomorrow. Look forward to it.
Chisato: T-the day after tomorrow...? And you’re saying you’re reworking your entire plan now... Are you sure you’ll be okay?
Kaoru: Of course. Didn’t Shakespeare once say it?
Kaoru: “It is never too late to be what you might have been.”

1959_Kaoru Seta_R5 The Stage Curtain Rises

Gacha phrase: I shall play my very own "Kaoru Seta" version of Shakespeare...!

<That Person's Stage Pt. 1>

(Character: Kaoru Seta, Chisato Shirasagi, Maya Yamato)

[Residential Area]

Chisato: That photo shoot finished quicker than I thought. This means I’ll definitely make it to class on time.
Maya: Yes! Thanks to Chisato-san’s posing advice!
Chisato: Haha, and Maya-chan’s handling of the equipment trouble was incredibly smooth.
Kaoru: ...Fleeting...
Maya: Huh? Is that... over there...?
Kaoru: Oh, isn't it Chisato and Maya. Meeting you here—what an fleeting stroke of fortune...
Kaoru: It’s as if fate itself is blessing us...
Chisato: Haah... It’s just the road to the university, so of course we’d meet.
Maya: Haha... Kaoru-san, you’re even more poetic than usual today.
Maya: Did something good happen?
Kaoru: Ah, will you listen to my story? It’s like a fleeting daytime dream...
Kaoru: I’ve been cast as the lead role in our university theater class.
Maya: Wow, as expected of Kaoru-san! Have they decided on the script yet?
Kaoru: Actually... I suggested performing the life of Shakespeare, and it was accepted.
Kaoru: And I’ve been chosen to play the role of Shakespeare!
Maya: Shakespeare...! No wonder you’ve been looking extra happy today!
Chisato: Playing Shakespeare means you’ll be acting based on his biography, right?
Kaoru: Well, it’s for a theater class, so... we’re going to write the script together.
Kaoru: Right now, everyone in class is discussing it. We’re all talking about what kind of story to create.
Maya: Wow, that sounds really interesting!
Kaoru: Ah... Shakespeare lived such a fascinating life.
Kaoru: He was born into a wealthy mayor’s family, and he lived a comfortable, carefree life with kind parents.
Kaoru: But during his youth, his respected father fell into ruin... Ah, what a tragedy...!
Kaoru: But even through such hardships, Shakespeare overcame them all and rose to greatness...!
Kaoru: What a fleeting yet strong person he was...
Chisato: I remember reading Shakespeare’s biography long ago, but the records from around his 20s are missing, aren’t they?
Kaoru: Ah! What a keen observation, Chisato...!
Kaoru: Actually, there’s a period in Shakespeare’s life, from when he was 20 years old until he became a playwright, where no records exist at all.
Kaoru: It’s called ‘The Lost Years.’
Kaoru: Later, there are accounts of him in London, but by then, he was already active as a playwright...
Maya: I see... So, does that mean you won’t include The Lost Years in the script?
Kaoru: Well, that’s what everyone initially said...
Kaoru: But between the time he got married and had three children and the moment he became a playwright... What hardships did he face, and what encounters did he have?
Kaoru: And why did he end up becoming a playwright in London... I thought we couldn’t portray Shakespeare without exploring that.
Chisato: So, you’re saying... you’ll also perform The Lost Years?
Kaoru: Exactly...! The script isn’t finished yet, but we’re all working on it together.
Maya: Haha, that sounds wonderful!
Kaoru: What could Shakespeare have been doing during The Lost Years? What do you think, Maya, Chisato?
Chisato: Hmm... I’m not entirely sure, but it’s certain he must have faced many struggles.
Maya: That’s true... He might have been smoldering in frustration, unable to be understood by anyone...
Kaoru: Ah, what a fleeting and delicate thought...
Kaoru: The Shakespeare I envision must have experienced constant conflict and suffering... And that’s why he was able to create such incredible tragic stories.
Kaoru: Feeling like he was nothing, struggling and wanting to escape... Maybe he thought like that too...
Kaoru: Just like I used to...
Chisato: Kaoru...
Kaoru: I promise to convey the Shakespeare I understand precisely because of who I am.
Maya: Yes, please do your best!
Kaoru: Thank you, Maya.
Kaoru: Well then, I must hurry back to the university. May fortune smile upon your journeys, Chisato and Maya...! Adieu!
Chisato: ...It sounds like fun.
Maya: Yes! Kaoru-san’s portrayal of Shakespeare is sure to be amazing.
Chisato: Acting out parts not in the biography... it must be difficult, but I’m sure Kaoru will pull it off.
Maya: Haha, that’s true!


<That Person's Stage Pt. 2>

(Character: Kaoru Seta, Maya Yamato)

[Residential Area]

Kaoru: Ah... I just want to run barefoot into the horizon and disappear...!
Kaoru: No. This won’t convey the searing pain of Shakespeare...
Kaoru: ......
Maya: ...san? Kaoru-san...?
Kaoru: Ah... Maya...
Maya: Are you okay? You’ve been standing there staring at the ground for a while...
Kaoru: Ah... actually, I’ve been troubled by the Shakespeare role we talked about the other day...
Maya: That’s why you had such a gloomy look...
Maya: But you were so excited about it. What happened? Is it the script? Is it not going well?
Kaoru: No, that’s not it. The script itself has turned out to be amazing.
Kaoru: If anything, it’s me... my acting... I’m not able to bring out the brilliance of this wonderful script.
Kaoru: As an actor, it’s my duty to fully bring out the charm of the script...
Maya: I see. So, you’re saying you’re not satisfied with your performance?
Kaoru: Yes, that’s exactly it, Maya...! No matter how many times I rehearse, even when I immerse myself in Shakespeare’s struggles and conflicts...
Kaoru: I still don’t feel like I’m truly expressing who Shakespeare was.
Maya: I see... It’s rare to see you so troubled, Kaoru-san...
Kaoru: I don’t truly know him. I only know Shakespeare through the stories he wrote.
Kaoru: Someone like me, who never knew the real Shakespeare, dared to wish to become him...
Kaoru: Ah, I feel so ashamed for being the one who suggested bringing my respected Shakespeare to the stage...
Maya: Kaoru-san... Your portrayal of Shakespeare is absolutely not wrong!
Kaoru: Maya... Haha, thank you for comforting me.
Maya: I’m not comforting you...!
Maya: Kaoru-san, you love Shakespeare more than anyone else. I know that very well!
Kaoru: Maya...
Maya: And... the fact that you’re so troubled you feel like running away, but you’re facing Shakespeare sincerely...
Maya: I think Shakespeare is alive within you, Kaoru-san!
Kaoru: Alive within me...? What do you mean?
Maya: Nobody truly knows what Shakespeare was like when he was alive.
Maya: But... for any role, it’s not just about reading the script. It’s about wondering, 'What kind of person was this?' Thinking, 'They must have felt this way,' isn’t it...?
Maya: I think it means that the role is already alive and moving within you.
Kaoru: Shakespeare is... alive within me... Maya... Is it okay for me to portray Shakespeare?
Maya: Of course it is! Look, even Shakespeare said, didn’t he?
Maya: Do not abandon the purpose you set out to achieve because of a single defeat.'
Kaoru: ......！
Kaoru: Maya...!
Maya: Haha... Kaoru-san, you can absolutely live as the Shakespeare within you.
Kaoru: Haha... That’s right... Theater is filled with each person’s life...
Kaoru: Somehow, I let myself get too caught up in Shakespeare and lost sight of myself.
Kaoru: I’ll portray Shakespeare in my own way... I shall play my very own "Kaoru Seta" version of Shakespeare...!
Maya: Yes! Please do your best!

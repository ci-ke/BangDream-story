<The Shape-Shifting Actor>

(Character: Kaoru Seta, Maya Yamato)

[Dressing Room]

Maya: Kaoru-san, great work on the performance.
Kaoru: Ah, Maya. You came! I spotted you in the audience from the stage.
Maya: Haha, as expected of you, Kaoru-san. I happened to have a meeting nearby, so I dropped by afterward.
Kaoru: What...! Even with how busy Pastel＊Palettes is, you still made time to come see me...!?
Kaoru: Maya... I can’t express how grateful I am. But allow me to say it anyway—thank you...!
Maya: Not at all...! I should be thanking you for showing me something amazing.
Kaoru: If that’s how you felt, then I’m deeply moved. Does that mean you enjoyed the story we brought to life?
Maya: Yes! Your performance was incredible, of course, but the staging and set design were also fantastic.
Kaoru: I see, the staging and design... very you, Maya. Would you mind telling me how it came across to your eyes?
Maya: Let’s see... I was especially impressed by the subtle color shifts in the lighting that represented the aurora, and the use of scrims in the staging.
Maya: Your eye movements matched the lighting shifts perfectly, and it truly felt like a real aurora was there on stage...
Maya: Even though I knew there was no real aurora, I couldn’t help but glance upward too.
Maya: As someone who’s worked backstage, it was all incredibly interesting.
Kaoru: Fufu...! As expected of Maya. Such sharp observation and analysis.
Kaoru: Of course, everything is designed so that all our guests can enjoy the show.
Kaoru: But when someone like you understands the intention behind the direction and design, it really makes it worth the effort as an actor.
Maya: Oh no...! I’m just someone who can’t help but see the stage through a backstage lens.
Maya: Ah, and one more thing...
Kaoru: Hm? Did something catch your attention?
Maya: No, it’s not that something bothered me... It’s just, the role you played this time...
Kaoru: Ah, the girl from a village with an aurora legend, who doesn’t believe in the legend and is a bit cynical... a truly rewarding role to play. Why do you ask?
Maya: I realized that it was a role you never played back in our high school theater club days.
Kaoru: Yeah, you're right. Back then, I often played heroic roles like knights or princes who protect princesses.
Maya: Yes. I remember everyone at Haneoka’s theater club wanted to see you in those cool roles.
Maya: Even your fans were hoping for that, and honestly, I also thought those roles suited you best.
Kaoru: Fufu, answering the voices of the little kittens is both my mission and my joy.
Maya: You always brought the characters to life—just as they were meant to be... no, even beyond that. That’s why I always thought you were an incredible actor, Kaoru-san.
Maya: But... after seeing today’s performance, I realized you’re an even more amazing actor than we thought. You can bring any role to life.
Maya: Thinking that makes me wish we’d had you try more roles back in high school... I kind of regret it.
Kaoru: ...I see, so that’s how you feel.
Kaoru: Yeah... you’re right. I guess I did end up playing a lot of similar roles in the theater club back in high school.
Kaoru: But just because they were similar doesn’t mean they were the same. Every stage, every role I played, was one of a kind.
Maya: ...!
Kaoru: Being able to play many different roles on various stages now is a real joy.
Kaoru: But each of the roles I played during my Haneoka days has absolutely contributed to who I am now.
Kaoru: If you see today’s Kaoru Seta as a great actor...
Kaoru: Then it’s thanks to you and everyone in the Haneoka theater club who helped shape me.
Maya: Kaoru-san...
Maya: ...You’re right. A stage isn’t made by one person alone.
Maya: Actors, crew, and the audience—only with all of them together does a performance come to life.
Kaoru: Yeah, exactly. And that’s the true joy of the stage.
Maya: Huhehe... hearing all this makes me even more excited to see the next role you’ll play.
Kaoru: Yes, I hope you look forward to it. Kaoru Seta will continue striving to reach ever greater heights.
Maya: Yeah. I’ll be looking forward to your next performance.
Kaoru: Fufu. I shall answer that hope with all the fleeting beauty I can offer.
Maya: ...But first, it’s important to see this current show through to the final performance.
Kaoru: Yes, you’re right. I’ll give it my all in the upcoming shows too, for all the little kittens who are looking forward to them.

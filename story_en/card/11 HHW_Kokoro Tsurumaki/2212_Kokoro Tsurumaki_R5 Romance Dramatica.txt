<A Two-Only Ending Pt. 1>

(Character: Kokoro Tsurumaki, Kaoru Seta)

[Tsurumaki Residence]

Kokoro: Kaoru! I’ve been waiting for you—welcome!
Kaoru: Hey, Kokoro. Am I the first to arrive today?
Kokoro: Yes! But I’m sure everyone else will be here soon!
Kokoro: I wonder what we should talk about at today’s Hello, Happy World! meeting. Maybe our next live show?
Kaoru: Sounds good. I’m sure it’ll be a fleeting yet beautiful moment—
Kaoru: Oh? A message on my phone...
Kokoro: Oh my...? Is something the matter?
Kaoru: ...Ah, Kokoro. I’m afraid I bring unfortunate news. It seems everyone else got caught up with something.
Kokoro: Oh! Then it’s just you and me today, Kaoru.
Kaoru: Fufu, so it seems. Well then, since it’s just the two of us—how shall we spend this precious time?
Kokoro: Then let’s play together! What should we do?
Kaoru: Let’s see... ah, that reminds me—I brought something good.
Kaoru: I actually just received the script for our next performance the other day.
Kokoro: Oh, what kind of story is it this time?
Kaoru: It’s a stage adaptation of a famous film—a bittersweet love story between a princess of a certain country and a freelance writer.
Kaoru: I’ve only skimmed through it, but it’s quite an intriguing tale.
Kokoro: That sounds like such a wonderful story! Now I want to read it too!
Kaoru: I thought you might say that. I was actually hoping to do a bit of reading practice for rehearsal.
Kaoru: I’ll play the writer’s role—would you read the princess’s lines for me?
Kokoro: Of course! Let’s get started right away!
(Black cut)
Kaoru: ...“My lady. I must bid you farewell.”
Kokoro: “Why would you say something like that?”
Kokoro: “I don’t want to say goodbye to you!”
Kaoru: “Ah, my lady. please, say no more. You and I live in different worlds...”
Kaoru: “You are a princess of this kingdom, bound by royal duty. You are not like me.”
Kokoro: “Stop it! Don’t say such things!”
Kokoro: “If you say that, I’ll—”
Kokoro: I’ll...?
Kaoru: What’s wrong, Kokoro?
Kokoro: This is really fun, reading lines like this—but somehow, it feels different.
Kokoro: Different from you, Kaoru—and from the way other actors perform.
Kaoru: Fufu, everyone has their own way of acting. Each interpretation is unique, and that’s what makes it special.
Kaoru: But I think your honest, straightforward acting is truly wonderful, Kokoro.
Kokoro: Thank you, Kaoru! But I want to read it like you.
Kokoro: When you read your lines, it sounds like the words are really your own.
Kaoru: Thank you. For an actor, there’s no higher praise than that.
Kokoro: How can I read like you do? If I could, I think it would be so much fun!
Kaoru: Hmm... let’s see... My way of acting, huh...
Kaoru: For example, how about trying to follow the character’s emotions?
Kokoro: Follow their emotions? How do I do that?
Kaoru: Well, when you read the princess’s lines—what did you feel?
Kokoro: Let’s see... I don’t think it’s a happy thing for the two of them to part ways.
Kokoro: Because they clearly love each other, right? So why do they have to say goodbye?
Kaoru: That’s a natural question. Two people who love each other should be together to be happy.
Kaoru: But there’s a difference in their social standing, and the princess has duties she must fulfill. That’s why they can’t stay together...
Kaoru: The characters in this story both understand that.
Kokoro: I see. then doesn’t that mean neither of them will be happy?
Kaoru: You’re right—it’s a tale of tragic love, where two people part ways despite their feelings.
Kaoru: You untangle each of their feelings, one by one, and think as if you were that person yourself...
Kaoru: ...By tracing the characters’ emotions in that way, you can create a more genuine, lifelike performance.
Kokoro: I get it. Let me try!
Kaoru: Alright, let’s pick up from where we left off.
Kokoro: (If I were the princess...)
Kaoru: “My lady. You understand this too, don’t you?”
Kokoro: (If I were the princess, what would I say back?)
Kaoru: “We can no longer be together. This is where we part.”
Kokoro: !! My next line is...
Kokoro: “If you do that, I’ll die of sorrow!”
Kokoro: ...Oh? I said it the same way as before, didn’t I?
Kaoru: Haha, there’s no need to rush. I’ll say it again—your natural way of acting is wonderful, Kokoro.
Kokoro: Thank you! You’re so kind, Kaoru!
Kokoro: But chasing after a character’s feelings—it’s so difficult, and that makes it so exciting!
Kaoru: Yeah. To relive the emotions of someone other than yourself... It's a truly moving thing to do.
Kokoro: You’re right! I want to chase the princess’s feelings even more!
Kokoro: Let’s read together again sometime! Before then, I’ll think lots and lots about how she feels!


<A Two-Only Ending Pt. 2>

(Character: Kokoro Tsurumaki, Kaoru Seta)

[A Few Days Later]

[Castle]

Kaoru: This place is...
Kokoro: It’s a castle! Isn’t it beautiful and amazing?
Kaoru: It certainly is beautiful, but why did you suddenly bring me to a place like this?
Kokoro: Of course, we’re going to perform here!
Kaoru: Perform?
Kokoro: Yes! Remember when we did that play together the other day?
Kokoro: After that, I kept trying to figure out how the princess must have felt in my own way! But no matter what, I just couldn't understand...
Kaoru: I see. But then, why here...?
Kokoro: Don’t you think this place perfectly fits the setting for the final scene?
Kaoru: Ah... you’re right. It might even feel more real than the actual stage set.
Kokoro: Exactly! I thought maybe here, I could get closer to the princess’s true feelings!
Kaoru: ...I see, so that’s it.
Kaoru: Fufu, I never imagined you’d think so deeply about acting, Kokoro...
Kaoru: Thank you, Kokoro. With this, I’m sure we’ll have a wonderful rehearsal.
Kokoro: No need to thank me! Now, let’s begin!
(Black cut)
Kokoro: Kaoru~! Is this spot okay~!?
Kaoru: Yes, that looks perfect.
Kaoru: The princess stands near the top of the stairs... and the freelance writer gazes up from below...
Kaoru: I see, so that’s what it looks like in reality... Yes, such a fleeting sense of distance.
Kokoro: Once the princess climbs these stairs, they’ll never see each other again, right?
Kaoru: That’s right. The princess ascends the grand staircase—disappearing from the writer’s view, symbolizing their eternal farewell.
Kaoru: It’s a sorrowful, fleeting scene that shows their lives will never cross again.
Kokoro: Got it. Let’s try it out right away!
Kaoru: Ah, indeed. Then, let us begin.
Kaoru: ...“Once she disappears beyond these stairs... I’ll never see her again.”
Kaoru: “But it can’t be helped. She and I belong to two entirely different worlds.”
Kokoro: “Farewell.”
Kaoru: “From here on, she and I will walk separate paths.”
Kaoru: “...But still... I’ll never forget that dazzling summer afternoon we spent racing through the city together.”
Kaoru: “I know I’ll carry that memory with me forever.”
Kokoro: “My prince...”
Kokoro: ......
Kokoro: ~~~No way! Absolutely not!!!
Kaoru: Eh!?
Kokoro: No! Absolutely not! We can’t just say goodbye like that!
Kokoro: We have to stay together—take each other’s hands and laugh, just like that day!
Kokoro: You can’t make such a sad face—both of us need to keep smiling!!
Kaoru: ...!!
Kokoro: Kaoru, I really tried my best to understand how the princess felt. But no matter how many times I think about it, I just can’t get it.
Kokoro: The more I think about it, the more I realize how much I love you, so I just can’t understand why she would choose to say goodbye!
Kaoru: Kokoro...!
Kokoro: If it were me, I’d run down these stairs—to be by his side!
Kokoro: I’d stay with him forever, never letting go. The two of us would hold hands and live happily ever after!
Kokoro: Because if we don’t, no one can be happy. Don’t you think so?
Kaoru: ...Heh.
Kaoru: Fufu... yes, you’re absolutely right...
Kokoro: Hmm? Did I say something strange?
Kokoro: But look—you’re finally smiling again, Mr. Writer!
Kaoru: Yes... thanks to you, my princess.
Kaoru: You truly are a princess who can turn even tragedy into a happy ending.
(White cut)
